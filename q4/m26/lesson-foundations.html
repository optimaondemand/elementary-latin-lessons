📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Remember our Roman numbers? They are still all around us!
A while back, you learned some special number shapes the Romans used: I, V, and X. Today we say hello to them again — and we look for them in places you might not expect, like clocks!
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can read the Roman numerals I, II, III, IV, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, and X.
2
I can point out L and C as two more numeral shapes I might spot.
3
I can look for a Roman numeral somewhere in my own home.

▶

On many clocks, the number 4 is written IIII instead of IV. Nobody knows for sure why — it's a real Roman-numeral mystery you can go spot on clocks at home!

Now you try: which numeral shape matches the dots?

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THE DOTS
Tap the numeral shape that matches the dots.
🤗 NEED HELP?
Counting dots one at a time works every time. Point to each dot as you count, then match your number to I, V, or X. Take all the time you need — there is no clock running on you!
▶  Go Further: hunt for a clock at home
If there is a clock at home with numeral shapes on it, look closely at the 4. Does it say IIII or IV? Either way is normal — now you know the secret!
