Where do Roman numerals still show up today?
Back in Module 3 and Module 9, you learned to read I through X, plus L and C. Rome itself is long gone, but its number system never really left. Today you'll go looking for Roman numerals hiding in the modern world — on clocks, in movie titles, in royal names, and even at the biggest football game of the year.

🔎 PART 2 · READING: STILL COUNTING THE ROMAN WAY
Roman numerals never stayed only in ancient Rome. Today you can find them on clock faces — many clocks use IIII instead of IV for the number 4, and nobody is completely sure why, but it's a real pattern you can go spot for yourself. Movie series count the same old way: the original Star Wars films are officially numbered Episode IV, V, and VI, and the Rocky movies climb from II to V. Kings and queens carry Roman numerals in their names too, like Queen Elizabeth II and King Henry VIII, and every Super Bowl gets its own numeral — Super Bowl LVIII was played in 2024.
