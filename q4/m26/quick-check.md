# Module 26 — Quick Check (Session 2)
**Topic:** Roman Numerals in Today's World · Q4 · Rome's Legacy

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (Completion-Based)

**Format:** Canvas Assignment, completion-graded only (no auto-grading, no numeric score beyond completion credit).

**Title:** Module 26 Quick Check — Numeral Picture Match

**Description for students:** A picture-based matching activity reviewing the numeral shapes I, II, III, V, and X. Students match each numeral shape to a picture card showing the matching number of dots (or matching set of pictures), the same style of matching used in the Session 1 lesson. No independent reading is required — all prompts are picture/audio-led, and the activity is untimed.

**Suggested structure (5 pairs):**
1. Numeral card **I** ↔ picture card showing 1 dot/object
2. Numeral card **II** ↔ picture card showing 2 dots/objects
3. Numeral card **III** ↔ picture card showing 3 dots/objects
4. Numeral card **V** ↔ picture card showing 5 dots/objects
5. Numeral card **X** ↔ picture card showing 10 dots/objects

**Grading:** Completion only. A student who attempts the matching activity earns full completion credit regardless of number of correct matches on the first try — this is a review/practice check, not a mastery gate.

**Teacher note:** L and C are intentionally NOT included in this completion assignment; Foundations students only need light shape-recognition of L/C from the lesson itself, not graded matching.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (Auto-Graded, 5 Questions)

**Format:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, 4 options each, best of 2 attempts.

**Title:** Module 26 Quick Check — Roman Numerals in Today's World

**Question 1 (numeral recognition):**
What number does the Roman numeral **X** stand for?
- A) 1
- B) 5
- **C) 10** ✅
- D) 100

*Feedback (correct):* Right — X stands for 10.

**Question 2 (numeral recognition):**
What number does the Roman numeral **C** stand for?
- A) 5
- B) 50
- **C) 100** ✅
- D) 1

*Feedback (correct):* Yes — C stands for 100.

**Question 3 (real-world appearance — clocks):**
On many clock faces, the number 4 is often written as which of these?
- A) IV
- **B) IIII** ✅
- C) IX
- D) VI

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — many clocks show 4 as IIII instead of IV. It's a real, well-known clock-making pattern.

**Question 4 (real-world appearance — monarchs):**
Which of these is the correct way Queen Elizabeth II's name uses a Roman numeral?
- **A) Elizabeth II** ✅
- B) Elizabeth IV
- C) Elizabeth VI
- D) Elizabeth IX

*Feedback (correct):* Yes — Queen Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom carries the numeral II in her name.

**Question 5 (real-world appearance — Super Bowl / sequels):**
Which statement about Roman numerals in modern life is accurate?
- A) The NFL does not use Roman numerals for the Super Bowl.
- **B) The original Star Wars films are officially numbered with Roman numerals (Episode IV, V, VI).** ✅
- C) Book and encyclopedia volumes never use Roman numerals.
- D) King Henry VIII's name uses Arabic numbers, not Roman numerals.

*Feedback (correct):* Right — the original trilogy is officially titled Episode IV: A New Hope, Episode V: The Empire Strikes Back, and Episode VI: Return of the Jedi.

## Optional Bonus (Not Part of the Graded Quick Check)

**Numeral Scavenger Hunt** — an optional, ungraded-beyond-completion, take-home activity. Students look for Roman numerals they can find at home (clock faces, book spines, movie titles, remote controls, etc.) and either photograph or list what they find. This is explicitly optional and framed as a fun bonus. It is never required for Module 26 to be considered complete, and if a student does not submit it, there is no penalty. If submitted, award light completion credit only — do not grade for accuracy or quantity.
