# Module 29 Reflection — Heroes of Roman Myth (Hercules & Aeneas)

One-question Module Reflection wrapper. Delivered as the module's closing Canvas item, immediately after the Quick Check.

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken or Drawn (Completion-Based)

**Format:** Canvas Assignment, completion-graded only. No typed response required or expected.

**Prompt shown to students:**
"Tell a grown-up or draw: which hero's story did you like better today — Hercules or Aeneas — and why?"

**Submission:** Student either (a) tells a grown-up out loud, with no digital submission needed, or (b) uploads/describes a drawing if the teacher wants a light artifact of completion. Either path earns full credit; the point is spoken or drawn engagement, not written prose.

**Grading:** Completion only, marked done once the student confirms they completed the conversation or drawing (e.g., a single "I did it!" checkbox or short completion note, per standard Foundations wrapper convention).

---

## Primary (3-5) — Typed (1-2 Sentences)

**Format:** Canvas Assignment (short text entry), 1-2 sentences, completion/effort-graded per standard Primary reflection convention (not auto-graded, not scored for "correct" content).

**Prompt shown to students:**
"What might *virtūs* — courage, or a hero's 'manly excellence' — have meant to Hercules or Aeneas in their own stories? Answer in one or two sentences."

**Grading:** Full credit for a genuine, on-topic 1-2 sentence response referencing virtūs and at least one of the two heroes. No single "correct" answer is expected; students may reasonably connect virtūs to Hercules's strength in facing the lion and Hydra, or to Aeneas's persistence across a long sea journey.
