# Module 29 Quick Check — Heroes of Roman Myth (Hercules & Aeneas)

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (Completion-Based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion-graded only (no auto-grading; no "correct answer" tracked in the gradebook).

**Title:** Module 29 Quick Check — Which Hero Is It?

**Description shown to students:** "Play the hero-and-picture matching game below. Match each picture to the hero it belongs to: Hercules (the strong hero who fought a lion and a many-headed serpent) and Aeneas (the hero who sailed across the sea to Italy). Play as many times as you like, then mark this assignment done!"

**Activity:** A simple picture-matching game (the same `.ow-select` widget embedded in the Foundations lesson, Part 3, or a standalone re-embed for the assignment page). Two rounds:
1. 🦁 (fierce lion) → matches **Hercules**
2. ⛵ (ship on the sea) → matches **Aeneas**

Optionally, a teacher may add one or two more picture rounds for extra practice (e.g., a coiling serpent 🐍 icon also matching Hercules), but the two above are the required minimum and are fully sourced from verified content.

**Grading:** Completion only. Students receive full credit for opening and attempting the matching game at least once. No score is calculated from correct/incorrect picks — the widget's in-lesson feedback (see lesson-foundations.html Part 3) is the only feedback loop, and it is purely instructional, not punitive.

**Teacher note:** This assignment does not require typed or written output from students. It pairs with the spoken/drawn reflection in reflection.md, which is the graded completion item capturing student engagement with today's content.
