📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
What made someone a hero to the Romans?
The Romans told stories about heroes, larger-than-life figures known for courage and great deeds. Today you'll meet two of the most famous: Hercules, known across the ancient world for his incredible strength, and Aeneas, a legendary hero connected to the story of Rome's own beginnings.

I can explain what the Latin words hērōs and virtūs meant to the Romans.
2

📜 PART 2 · READING: HEROES OF OLD ROME
In Latin, hērōs means "hero" (a word the Romans borrowed from the Greeks), and virtūs originally meant courage, or a hero's manly excellence. Hercules showed great virtūs by defeating the fierce Nemean Lion and the many-headed serpent called the Hydra. Another legendary hero, Aeneas, was said to have escaped a great, ancient war, journeyed across the sea, and eventually reached Italy, where Roman legend connects his descendants to the later founding of Rome. You'll meet Aeneas again if you study Latin in high school; this is just your first hello.
