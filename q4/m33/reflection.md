Module Reflection — one question, celebratory year-end framing. This module still gets its own reflection wrapper even though the Roman Passport Showcase (see roman-passport-showcase.md) replaces the usual quick check.

## Foundations (K-2)

Spoken/drawn prompt:

"Tell a grown-up or draw: what is one thing about ancient Rome you will remember forever?"

No typing required. A student may point to something in their Roman Passport, say it out loud to a grown-up, or draw a quick picture. Any genuine answer is a complete answer — this is a celebration of what stuck with them, not a check for a "right" fact.

## Primary (3-5)

Typed prompt, 1-2 sentences:

"What was your single favorite thing you learned about the Roman world this year, and why?"

Students type a short, personal answer — a favorite god, a fact about Roman daily life, a piece of Roman engineering, or a Latin word they loved. The tone should stay warm and personal, not a formal review of the year's content; there is no single correct answer, and light completion-based feedback is all that's expected.
