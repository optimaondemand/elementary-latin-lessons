# Module 32 Reflection — Cumulative Review Part 1: Numbers, Days & Months

Module Reflection wrapper (Canvas). One question, referenced in each lane's "What You'll Do Next" section.

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken/Drawn Reflection

**Format:** Spoken to a grown-up, or drawn. Not typed. Untimed. No independent reading required.

**Prompt:** "Tell a grown-up or draw: which was more fun to learn this year — the numbers, the days, or the months — and why?"

**Teacher note:** This is a completion-based, low-stakes wrap-up. There is no right answer; the goal is a moment of student-led recall and personal reflection on a full year of vocabulary. No typed submission is expected at this grade band.

---

## Primary (3-5) — Typed Reflection

**Format:** Typed, 1-2 sentences, submitted as a short Canvas text-entry response.

**Prompt:** "Explain the September–October–November–December calendar puzzle in your own words: why do these four month names come from the Latin words for seven, eight, nine, and ten, even though they're now the ninth through twelfth months?"

**Grading:** Completion/effort-based (short written response), not correctness-scored — students should demonstrate they can restate the historical fact (the earliest Roman calendar began in March, not January) in their own words, at whatever level of detail a 3rd-5th grader can manage.
