📜 PART 2 · READING: A WHOLE YEAR OF LATIN
Think back over everything you have learned this year. You can count from ūnus all the way to vīgintī, and you know the Latin words for fifty (L) and one hundred (C). You know all seven day names, each one named for the Sun, Moon, or a Roman god. And you know all twelve month names — including a strange fact hiding inside four of them. September, October, November, and December come from the Latin words for seven, eight, nine, and ten — even though they are now the ninth, tenth, eleventh, and twelfth months. That mismatch happened because the earliest Roman calendar began in March, not January.

The Calendar Puzzle, laid out:

7️⃣
Septem
"seven" → September, now month 9
8️⃣
Octō
"eight" → October, now month 10
9️⃣
Novem
"nine" → November, now month 11
🔟
Decem
"ten" → December, now month 12
