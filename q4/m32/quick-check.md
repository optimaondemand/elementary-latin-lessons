## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (Auto-Graded, 5 Questions)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, 4 options each. Best of 2 attempts.

**Title:** Module 32 Quick Check

1. **What does the Latin number *vīgintī* mean?**
   A. twelve
   B. **twenty** ✅
   C. fifty
   D. one hundred
   *Feedback (correct): Right — vīgintī is Latin for twenty.*

2. **The Roman numeral C stands for which number?**
   A. ten
   B. fifty
   C. **one hundred** ✅
   D. twenty
   *Feedback (correct): Correct — C is the Roman numeral for one hundred.*

3. **Which day of the week is *dies Iovis*, the "day of Jupiter"?**
   A. Sunday
   B. Tuesday
   C. Wednesday
   D. **Thursday** ✅
   *Feedback (correct): Yes — dies Iovis, day of Jupiter, is Thursday.*

4. **Which day of the week is *dies Veneris*, the "day of Venus"?**
   A. **Friday** ✅
   B. Saturday
   C. Monday
   D. Tuesday
   *Feedback (correct): Right — dies Veneris, day of Venus, is Friday.*

5. **The month name October comes from the Latin word for which number?**
   A. seven
   B. **eight** ✅
   C. nine
   D. ten
   *Feedback (correct): Correct — October comes from octō, Latin for eight, even though October is now the tenth month.*

**Sourcing note:** All five questions draw only from the verified content list for this module (numbers ūnus–vīgintī plus L/C; the seven day names tied to Sun/Moon/Roman gods; the twelve month names and the September–December calendar-shift fact). No new vocabulary is introduced.
