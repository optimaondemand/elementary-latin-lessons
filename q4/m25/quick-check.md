## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (Completion-Based)

**Format:** Canvas Assignment, graded on completion only — no correctness grading.

**What's shown to the student:** Three picture cards, each showing one of the emoji/icon images used in the lesson —

- 🏛️ (the forum)
- 🗣️ (the senātus / Senate)
- 🤝 (rēspūblica)

— alongside three word/description cards:

- "Forum" — a giant outdoor meeting-and-market place in the middle of the city
- "Senātus" — a group of Roman leaders who met and took turns talking to decide what was best for the city
- "Rēspūblica" — the idea that the city belonged to everyone, not just to one king

The student drags or taps to match each picture card to its correct word/description card (same picture-matching format as the in-lesson widget, reused here as the graded version).

**What "complete" looks like:** The student has attempted a match for all three picture cards and submitted the activity. There is no minimum number of correct matches required — the assignment is marked complete for engaging with all three matches, not for getting them right. Teachers may spot-check accuracy informally but the Canvas gradebook entry itself is completion-only (full credit for submission).

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (Auto-Graded, 5 Questions)

**Format:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, 5 questions, best of 2 attempts.

**Question 1**
What was the forum in ancient Rome?
- A) A type of toga worn by senators
- B) A large open public square where Romans gathered to talk, shop, and conduct business ✅ **(correct)**
- C) A Roman law court building
- D) A famous Roman road

Feedback (correct): "Yes! The forum was the public square at the center of Roman life."

**Question 2**
What was the senātus (Senate)?
- A) A group of Roman leaders who met to discuss and decide things for the city ✅ **(correct)**
- B) A festival held every year in Rome
- C) The Roman army's commanding officers
- D) A school for young Roman citizens

Feedback (correct): "Right! The Senate was made up of Roman leaders who met to help govern the city."

**Question 3**
What does rēspūblica mean?
- A) "The emperor's palace"
- B) "The city gates"
- C) "A soldier's oath"
- D) "The public thing" — the idea that the city belonged to the people ✅ **(correct)**

Feedback (correct): "Correct! Rēspūblica meant the city and its decisions belonged to everyone, not one king."

**Question 4**
Which English word comes directly from the Latin word senātus?
- A) Senate ✅ **(correct)**
- B) Sentence
- C) Sentiment
- D) Senior

Feedback (correct): "Yes! Senate comes directly from senātus."

**Question 5**
For part of its history, before it became an empire ruled by emperors, what kind of government did Rome have?
- A) A monarchy ruled by a single king only
- B) A democracy where every citizen voted directly on all laws
- C) A republic, not ruled by a single king ✅ **(correct)**
- D) No government at all

Feedback (correct): "Right! Rome was a republic for part of its history, before later becoming an empire."
