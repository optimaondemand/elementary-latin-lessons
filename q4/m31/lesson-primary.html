📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Does ancient Rome still exist?
All year, you've studied Rome as it was long ago — tum, "then." But Rome did not vanish. Rome is a real, living city today — nunc, "now" — the capital of modern Italy, full of cars, apartments, shops, and millions of people, standing right alongside its own ancient ruins. Today you compare Rome then and Rome now.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can use tum (then) and nunc (now) to compare ancient and modern Rome.
2
I can explain how modern Rome is a real, living city that exists right alongside its ancient ruins.
3
I can match an ancient Roman thing to its modern-day equivalent.

🗺️ PART 2 · READING: A CITY LIVING IN TWO TIMES
Tum, long ago, Rome was the busy capital of a great empire. Nunc, today, Rome is still a real, living city — the capital of modern Italy — full of cars, apartment buildings, shops, and millions of people going about ordinary modern lives. What makes Rome unusual is that its ancient past never got cleared away: ruins like the Colosseum and the Roman Forum still stand in the middle of the modern city, so a person can walk from a bus stop straight into a two-thousand-year-old ruin. Tum and nunc exist side by side in Rome, not one after the other.
🟥 = Rome, still the capital of Italy today
