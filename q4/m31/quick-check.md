# Module 31 Quick Check — Rome Then and Now

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (Completion-Based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion-graded only (no correct/incorrect scoring).

**Title:** Module 31 Quick Check — Then-and-Now Picture Match

**Directions to student:** Look at each pair of pictures/icons below. Tap or point to show which "now" picture matches the "then" picture. Play as many times as you like — this is just for fun and practice!

**Format:** A simple picture/icon matching activity (3 pairs), reusing the same then-and-now pairs introduced in the Foundations lesson so students see the content twice before moving on:
1. Roman stone road (📜🛣️) → paved highway today (🛣️)
2. Roman aqueduct carrying water (📜💧) → modern water pipes (🚰)
3. The Colosseum where Romans watched shows (📜🏛️) → the Colosseum ruins people visit today (📸)

**[TEACHER NOTE: Real photographs are not yet available for this activity. Icon/emoji and short text labels are used as placeholders for each "then" and "now" pair. When real, rights-cleared photos are sourced — for example, an illustration or public-domain image of the ancient Colosseum paired with a real modern photograph of the Colosseum ruins — substitute them here for a more concrete then-and-now visual comparison.]**

**Grading:** Completion only. Any good-faith attempt (including a wrong first tap that the student corrects, or simply playing through all three pairs) earns full credit. No right/wrong score is recorded.

**Teacher time estimate:** Under 1 minute per student to confirm completion; no per-question grading required.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (Auto-Graded, 5 Questions)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, 4 options each, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What does the Latin word *nunc* mean?
- A. Then
- B. **Now** ✅
- C. Never
- D. Always

*Feedback (correct):* Right — nunc means "now," today's time.

**Q2.** What does the Latin word *tum* mean?
- A. **Then** ✅
- B. Now
- C. Soon
- D. Always

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — tum means "then," a long time ago.

**Q3.** The Colosseum in Rome today is best described as which of the following?
- A. A brand-new stadium built to replace the old one
- B. A building that was completely torn down and no longer exists
- C. **A famous ruin that still stands and that tourists visit today** ✅
- D. A modern apartment building

*Feedback (correct):* Yes — the Colosseum still stands today as a famous ruin people can visit and walk through.

**Q4.** Which statement is true about the ancient Roman roads and aqueducts mentioned in this module?
- A. No trace of them exists anywhere in Italy today
- B. They were rebuilt from scratch last century
- C. **Some ancient Roman roads and aqueduct remains can still be seen in Italy today** ✅
- D. They were only ever used in cities outside of Italy

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — some ancient Roman roads and aqueduct remains are still visible in and around Italy today, even though modern Italy now uses paved highways and modern plumbing.

**Q5.** Which of these is still true about Rome today?
- A. Rome is now an abandoned ruin with no one living there
- B. Rome moved to a different location in Italy
- C. Rome no longer exists on any map
- D. **Rome is a real, living modern city — the capital of Italy — with millions of people living ordinary modern lives** ✅

*Feedback (correct):* Right — Rome is still a real, living city today, the capital of modern Italy, existing right alongside its ancient ruins.
