Tum and Nunc: Then and Now in Rome

📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Some things from ancient Rome are still here — just different!
All year we have learned about Rome long, long ago. But guess what — the city of Rome is still there RIGHT NOW! Today we learn two new Latin words, tum (then) and nunc (now), and we compare old Rome to Rome today.
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say tum (then) and nunc (now).
2
I can match a picture from ancient Rome to what it looks like today.
3
I can tell or draw one thing from ancient Rome that is still around today.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED
▶
Headphones or speakers
▶
Paper and crayons or markers, for drawing
▶
A grown-up nearby is helpful but not required
⚡ PART 1 · LET'S BEGIN
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Do you think anything from a long, long time ago could still be around today?
🗺️ PART 2 · THEN AND NOW WORDS

Rome is a real city in Italy, and people live there right now! Here are two new words for comparing old Rome to Rome today.

📜
Tum
it means "then" — a long, long time ago
🏙️
Nunc
it means "now" — today
🟥 = Rome, still sitting in the same spot in Italy today!
👋 PART 3 · THEN-AND-NOW MATCHING GAME

Some things from ancient Rome (tum) turned into something a little different today (nunc). Match each old thing to what it turned into!

[TEACHER NOTE: This matching game currently uses icons and simple text labels in place of real photographs. When available, substitute an actual photo pair for each row — for example, a real photo of the Colosseum ruins today alongside an illustration of the ancient Colosseum.]

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THEN TO NOW
Tap the picture that shows what this Roman thing looks like today.
📜🛣️ Tum: a Roman road made of fitted stone
📜💧 Tum: a Roman aqueduct that carried water into the city
📜🏛️ Tum: the Colosseum, where Romans once watched shows
🤗 NEED HELP?
Three matches is plenty for one day! If a match feels tricky, say the word tum for the old picture and nunc for the new one out loud — that can help you decide.
▶  Go Further: look for a real photo with a grown-up
If a grown-up can help you search the internet, look up "Colosseum today" together and see a real photo of it still standing in Rome!
🎮 EXTRA PRACTICE · PLAY & EXPLORE

Let's play! Drag each Latin word to what it means. Play as many times as you like!

➡️ WHAT YOU'LL DO NEXT
1
Module 31 Quick Check
A fun then-and-now picture matching game. Play it as many times as you like!
2
Tell a Grown-Up or Draw
Tell a grown-up or draw a picture: what is one thing from ancient Rome that is still around today, just in a different form? That's your reflection!
💪 GREAT START

You just learned tum and nunc and matched Rome then to Rome now! Rome's story does not stop in the past — it is still being written today.
