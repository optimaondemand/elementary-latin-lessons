# Module 31 Reflection — Rome Then and Now

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken or Drawn Reflection

**Type:** Module Reflection wrapper, one question, not typed. Delivered as a Canvas assignment/module outro page that instructs the student to complete the reflection away from the keyboard (spoken to a grown-up, or drawn on paper), with a simple completion checkbox or upload of the drawing as the only submission requirement.

**Prompt (as shown to student):**
> Tell a grown-up or draw: what is one thing from ancient Rome that is still around today, just in a different form?

**Directions to student:** You do not need to type anything for this reflection. Either tell a grown-up your answer out loud, or draw a picture of your answer on paper. If you drew a picture, you may take a photo of it and upload it — but talking about it out loud is just as good.

**Grading:** Completion-based only. No right or wrong answer; any good-faith response (spoken or drawn) referencing something from today's then-and-now comparisons (the road, the aqueduct, the Colosseum, or another idea the student connects to the theme) counts as complete.

## Primary (3-5) — Typed Reflection

**Type:** Module Reflection wrapper, one question, typed response (1-2 sentences), delivered as a Canvas assignment/module outro page with a short text-entry box.

**Prompt (as shown to student):**
> In 1-2 sentences: what surprised you most about something from ancient Rome still being visible or usable today?

**Directions to student:** Type 1-2 sentences answering the prompt. There is no single right answer — this is about your own reaction to today's lesson.

**Grading:** Completion-based only (submitted / not submitted, or a simple check for a good-faith 1-2 sentence response). No content is scored for correctness, and no sample answer is provided or revealed to students.
