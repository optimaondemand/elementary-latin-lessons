# Module 30 — Gods of Rome IV — Full Review
## Module Reflection wrapper specs (outro)

**Type (both lanes):** Canvas Quiz, survey mode, one question, completion-graded (Tier 4 — ungraded scaffolding / Tier 2 reflection data point). No custom code — native Canvas Quiz formatting only.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken or Drawn

**Format:** Canvas Quiz, survey mode, single question, ungraded (completion only). Because this lane never requires typed or independent reading responses, the question is answered out loud to a grown-up or as a drawing — not typed into Canvas.

**Question (survey/essay question type, marked "no correct answer" so it never blocks completion):**
"Tell a grown-up or draw: which Roman god was your favorite to learn about this year, and why?"

**Response instructions to student:** "You don't have to type anything long here. Tell a grown-up out loud, or draw a picture, then just click Submit to mark this done."

**Suggested Canvas setup:** A single Essay/short-answer survey question where the student (or a grown-up) types a few words confirming the conversation/drawing happened (e.g., "Talked to Mom about Neptune"), OR a completion-only Assignment with a file-upload option for a photo of the drawing if the family wants to submit one. Either satisfies the "referenced in What You'll Do Next" requirement without requiring independent typed reflection from a K-2 student.

---

## Primary (3-5) — Typed (1-2 sentences)

**Format:** Canvas Quiz, survey mode, single essay question, completion-graded (teacher may skim in aggregate; not individually scored for content).

**Question:**
"Which two gods' domains do you think are most connected or similar, and why? Answer in 1-2 sentences."

**Response instructions to student:** "There's no single right answer — just explain your thinking in a sentence or two. For example, you might connect two gods who share a similar kind of power, or who work together in the myths you've learned about."

**Grading:** Completion-based (submitted/not submitted). Not scored for correctness — this is a reflection, not a content check (that's what the Quick Check quiz is for).
