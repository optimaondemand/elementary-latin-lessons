# Module 27 Quick Check — Latin in Science

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (Completion-Based)

**Format:** Canvas Assignment, completion-graded only (no right/wrong scoring visible to student; teacher confirms the student attempted the activity).

**Title:** Match the Animal to Its Name

**Description for students:** Look at the three animal pictures. Drag (or tell a grown-up how to match) each animal picture to the meaning-tag that goes with it.

**Materials:** Three picture cards (reuse the emoji/picture cards from the lesson):
- 🧑 person → "Wise Human" (Homo sapiens)
- 🦖 dinosaur → "Tyrant Lizard King" (Tyrannosaurus rex)
- 🐺 wolf → "Dog + Wolf" (Canis lupus)

**Task:** A simple picture-sort/matching board with the three animal pictures on one side and the three meaning-tags on the other. The student (with a grown-up's help if needed) draws a line, drags a tile, or points to connect each animal to its matching meaning-tag. No spelling or independent reading is required — pictures and short meaning-tags only.

**Grading:** Completion-based. The assignment is marked complete when the student has submitted (or a grown-up has confirmed) that the matching activity was attempted, regardless of accuracy. No auto-grading, no numeric score shown to the student.

**Teacher note:** This assignment mirrors the in-lesson Part 3 matching widget so students see the same three animals twice in different formats before the reflection.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (Auto-Graded, 5 Questions)

**Format:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, 4 options each. Best of 2 attempts (matches lesson's "What You'll Do Next").

**Question 1**
What does the Latin word-part *homo* mean, as in *Homo sapiens*?
- A. Human being, man ✅
- B. House
- C. Whole
- D. Hollow

*Feedback (correct):* Right — homo is Latin for "human being, man," the first half of Homo sapiens.

**Question 2**
What does the Latin word *sapiens* mean, as in *Homo sapiens*?
- A. Silent
- B. Sharp
- C. Wise, knowing ✅
- D. Seed

*Feedback (correct):* Yes — sapiens is Latin for "wise," from the verb sapere, "to know, to be wise."

**Question 3**
The name *Tyrannosaurus* comes from two word-parts. What language are they from, and what do they mean?
- A. Latin; "titanic snake"
- B. Greek; "tyrant lizard" ✅
- C. Latin; "toothy shark"
- D. Greek; "tiny turtle"

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — tyranno- and -saurus both come from Greek, meaning "tyrant, absolute ruler" and "lizard."

**Question 4**
In the name *Tyrannosaurus rex*, what does the Latin word *rex* mean?
- A. A dog's name
- B. Road
- C. Rest
- D. King ✅

*Feedback (correct):* Right — rex is the Latin word for "king."

**Question 5**
The gray wolf's scientific name is *Canis lupus*. What do the two Latin words mean?
- A. Cat and fox
- B. Horse and deer
- C. Dog and wolf ✅
- D. Bird and bear

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — canis is Latin for "dog" and lupus is Latin for "wolf."

**Grading:** Auto-graded, 1 point per question, 5 points total. Best of 2 attempts recorded in gradebook.
