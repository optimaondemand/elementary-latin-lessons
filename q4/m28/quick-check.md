# Module 28 Quick Check — Latin in Everyday English (Motto Hunt)

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (Completion-Based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion-graded only (no auto-grading, no "correct answer" scoring).

**Title:** Module 28 Quick Check — Motto Match

**Directions to student (read aloud by a grown-up if needed):**
Play the motto matching game from today's lesson. Match each Latin saying to its picture and its meaning:
- ⭐ Ad Astra Per Aspera → "To the Stars Through Difficulties"
- ⬆️ Excelsior → "Ever Upward"
- 🪙 E Pluribus Unum → "Out of Many, One"

You can play as many times as you like. When you're done, mark this assignment complete.

**Grading:** Completion only. Full credit is awarded for attempting the matching activity — there is no minimum score. Do not withhold credit based on how many matches a student got right the first time; the goal at this age is exposure and fun, not mastery.

**Teacher note:** This pairs with the picture/word matching widget embedded in `lesson-foundations.html` (Part 3). No separate Canvas quiz is needed for this lane.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (Auto-Graded, 5 Questions)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, 5 questions, best of 2 attempts.

**Title:** Module 28 Quick Check — Latin Mottoes

---

**Question 1**
Which Latin motto means "Out of Many, One," and appears on the Great Seal of the United States and on U.S. money?

A) Sic Semper Tyrannis
B) E Pluribus Unum ✅
C) Excelsior
D) Dum Spiro Spero

*Feedback (correct):* Right — E Pluribus Unum has appeared on U.S. currency for more than two hundred years.

---

**Question 2**
Virginia's state motto is "Sic Semper Tyrannis." What does it mean in English?

A) To the Stars Through Difficulties
B) Ever Upward
C) Thus Always to Tyrants ✅
D) Light and Truth

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — Sic Semper Tyrannis appears on Virginia's state seal and flag.

---

**Question 3**
New York's state motto is a single Latin word: "Excelsior." What does it mean?

A) Ever Upward ✅
B) Truth
C) While I Breathe, I Hope
D) Out of Many, One

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — Excelsior means "Ever Upward" or "higher."

---

**Question 4**
Kansas's state motto is "Ad Astra Per Aspera." What does it mean?

A) Light and Truth
B) To the Stars Through Difficulties ✅
C) Thus Always to Tyrants
D) Out of Many, One

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — Ad Astra Per Aspera is on the Kansas state seal.

---

**Question 5**
South Carolina's state motto is "Dum Spiro Spero." What does it mean?

A) While I Breathe, I Hope ✅
B) Ever Upward
C) Truth
D) To the Stars Through Difficulties

*Feedback (correct):* Correct — Dum Spiro Spero is South Carolina's state motto.

---

**Teacher note:** All five questions are drawn strictly from the verified motto list for this module (E Pluribus Unum; Virginia — Sic Semper Tyrannis; New York — Excelsior; Kansas — Ad Astra Per Aspera; South Carolina — Dum Spiro Spero). No additional state or school mottoes were invented for distractors — Harvard's "Veritas" ("Truth") and Yale's "Lux et Veritas" ("Light and Truth") from the lesson passage are reused only as plausible distractor options, never as a correct answer to a state-motto question.
