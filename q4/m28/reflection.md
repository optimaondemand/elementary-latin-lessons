# Module 28 Reflection — Latin in Everyday English (Motto Hunt)

Module Reflection wrapper — one question, delivered per lane per this course's usual reflection wrapper pattern.

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken or Drawn

**Format:** Not typed. Student responds out loud to a grown-up, or draws a picture. No text-entry field required; if the Canvas wrapper needs a placeholder submission field, accept "Discussed with a grown-up" / an uploaded drawing / or a completion checkbox.

**Prompt:**
Tell a grown-up or draw a picture: if your class had its own short motto or saying, what would you want it to say?

**Teacher note:** No right answer. The goal is for the student to connect today's motto hunt to something personal and creative. Completion-based only.

## Primary (3-5) — Typed, 1-2 Sentences

**Format:** Short typed response field (Canvas Assignment or Quiz essay-type question), 1-2 sentences.

**Prompt:**
Which motto's meaning did you like best today, and why?

**Teacher note:** Any of the five verified mottoes covered in the lesson (E Pluribus Unum; Sic Semper Tyrannis; Excelsior; Ad Astra Per Aspera; Dum Spiro Spero) is an acceptable answer. Grade for completion/effort and on-topic response, not for a "correct" pick — this is a personal-response reflection, not a content check (the content check lives in the Quick Check quiz).
