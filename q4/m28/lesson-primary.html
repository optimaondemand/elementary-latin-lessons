📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why does a coin in your pocket have Latin written on it?
A motto is a short saying that stands for what a place or group believes in. Long after Rome fell, people kept choosing Latin for their mottoes — you'll find them on U.S. money, on state flags and seals, and on school crests. Today you'll go hunting for a few of the most famous ones and figure out what they really mean.

🪙 PART 2 · READING: OUT OF MANY, ONE
Turn over almost any U.S. coin and you can find three Latin words: E pluribus unum, meaning "Out of many, one." This motto has appeared on the Great Seal of the United States and on American money for more than two hundred years, reminding people that one country was formed out of many separate states. Mottoes like this are not only national — many individual states have their own. Virginia's state motto is Sic Semper Tyrannis ("Thus Always to Tyrants"), and Kansas chose Ad Astra Per Aspera ("To the Stars Through Difficulties") for its state seal.
