LESSON 1.03
Saludos y despedidas — Greetings & Goodbyes
Nine phrases that let you start and end a real conversation in Spanish.
👋 TODAY'S NEW THING
You already have the sounds. Today you get the words to open — and close — a conversation.

Yesterday you learned the five vowels. Every phrase below uses them, so read each one out loud with your clean Spanish vowels. A conversation has two ends: a way in (a greeting) and a way out (a goodbye). You'll learn both today, so you never get stuck.

💬 A NOTE ON BEING POLITE

Spanish has two ways to say "you." Use tú with friends and people your own age. Use usted with a teacher, an adult you don't know, or anyone you want to show respect. Across most of Latin America, usted is the normal, respectful default — when in doubt, use it. That's why ¿Cómo está usted? is a safe, polite way to ask how someone is.
