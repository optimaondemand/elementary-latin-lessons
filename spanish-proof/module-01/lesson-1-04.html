LESSON 1.04 · SPANISH IN YOUR WORLD
The Spanish Hiding in English
You speak more Spanish than you realize — it's in your words and on your maps.
🌍 TODAY'S BIG IDEA
Hundreds of everyday English words — and the names of whole US states — came straight from Spanish.

On Day 1 we promised you a head start. Here it is. When English-speaking settlers met the Spanish-speaking world, they borrowed thousands of words and kept them. Every one is a little piece of Spanish you already own. Read these out loud — they're all Spanish:

rodeoplazapatiofiestatacosalsamosquitocanyon

And it isn't just words. Look at a US map: Florida, Colorado, Nevada, California, Los Ángeles — all Spanish names, each one describing something. Time to find out what they really mean.

🎮 PLAY · GUESS WHERE IT CAME FROM

Take your best guess for each one, then read the reveal. You can't lose — every round teaches you something.

✅ APPLY IT
Now use what you just learned. Imagine you're reading a US map with new eyes.
A friend says "Nevada is a random name." Based on Spanish, what does it actually describe?
Four of these place names are literal Spanish descriptions. Which one is NOT — it came from a novel?
Your town has a "Town Plaza." Using Spanish, what is that space, really?
★ WHY THIS MATTERS

Spanish isn't a foreign language dropped into your world from far away — it helped build your world. The names of your state, your rivers, and your cities are Spanish. Learning the language is really just getting to know a story you've been living inside all along.
