📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
How do you build a stadium for tens of thousands of people with no modern machines?
The Colosseum was one of the ancient world's great engineering achievements: a massive oval stone stadium with tiered seating and a hidden network of passages beneath its arena floor. Just as impressive in its own way was the Circus Maximus, Rome's giant chariot-racing track and one of the most popular entertainment venues in the Roman world.

🏟️ PART 2 · READING: BUILT TO HOLD A CROWD
The Colosseum, also called the Flavian Amphitheatre, was a massive oval stone stadium completed in Rome around 80 AD. It could hold tens of thousands of spectators who came to watch public games and contests, and a hidden network of passages ran beneath its arena floor to move performers and equipment into place. Just as popular was the circus Maximus, Rome's great chariot-racing track, where teams in different colors raced their chariots at breakneck speed while huge crowds cheered them on.
