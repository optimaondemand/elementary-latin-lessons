📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
A giant stone stadium and a racetrack full of chariots!
In Rome there was a huge, oval, stone building called the Colosseum where crowds of people came to watch public games. Nearby was the Circus Maximus, a long oval racetrack where teams raced fast chariots while everyone cheered. Today we visit both!
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say lūdī (games) and circus (racetrack).
2
I can point to the seats and the floor of a big oval stadium.
3
I can draw or tell a grown-up which one I'd rather watch.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED
▶
Headphones or speakers
▶
A grown-up nearby is helpful but not required

🤗 NEED HELP?
Circus sounds a little like our English word "circus" — think of a racetrack shape, oval like a circle. Lūdī just means fun games and shows. Watch the video again and say both words along with it.
▶  Go Further: look at real Colosseum pictures with a grown-up
Ask a grown-up to look up a picture of the real Colosseum with you. It's still standing in Rome today, even though it's almost 2,000 years old!
