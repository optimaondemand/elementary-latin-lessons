# Module 14 Quick Check — The Colosseum & Roman Entertainment

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS NM.6.3, NH.6.3

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade)
**Title:** Module 14 Quick Check: Stadium & Racetrack Match-Up
**Instructions shown to student:** "Look at each picture and tap the word that matches. Play as many times as you like!"
**Activity description:** A picture-matching activity with two picture-word pairs. Picture 1 shows a big oval stone stadium filled with a cheering crowd (paired with the word "lūdī," meaning games); picture 2 shows a chariot racing around an oval track (paired with the word "circus," meaning racetrack). The student taps or drags the correct Latin word onto each picture. Both words have a spoken audio label so the activity does not require reading. Placing a word correctly gives an immediate visual cue (a checkmark or gentle highlight); an incorrect placement gives a gentle "try again" cue rather than a graded score. The activity is untimed, contains no depiction of combat or injury, and can be replayed without limit.
**Submission / completion criteria:** Marked complete once the student has attempted the full matching activity one time, regardless of whether every picture was matched correctly. No correctness grade is recorded.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions)
**Type:** Canvas Quiz, multiple choice, auto-graded
**Title:** Module 14 Quick Check: The Colosseum & Roman Entertainment
**Instructions shown to student:** "Answer these 5 questions about today's lesson on the Colosseum and the Circus Maximus. You have 2 attempts — your highest score will be recorded."

1. What was the Colosseum built to do?
   A. Store Rome's grain supply
   B. Hold tens of thousands of spectators for public games and contests
   C. House the Roman senate
   D. Serve as a giant water tank
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — the Colosseum was a huge stone stadium built to hold enormous crowds."

2. About when was the Colosseum completed?
   A. 80 BC
   B. 80 AD
   C. 800 AD
   D. 8 AD
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — the Colosseum was completed around 80 AD."

3. What ran beneath the Colosseum's arena floor?
   A. An underground river
   B. A network of passages used to move performers and equipment
   C. A giant furnace
   D. Empty stone rubble
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — a hidden network of passages ran beneath the arena floor."

4. What happened at the Circus Maximus?
   A. Senators debated new laws
   B. Chariot teams raced around an oval track for cheering crowds
   C. Farmers sold grain and vegetables
   D. Students studied Latin grammar
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — the Circus Maximus was Rome's great chariot-racing venue."

5. The English word "circus" comes directly from the Latin word circus, which originally meant&hellip;
   A. a stone archway
   B. a circle or oval track
   C. a group of senators
   D. a type of chariot
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — Latin circus originally meant a circle or oval track, like the racetrack itself."
