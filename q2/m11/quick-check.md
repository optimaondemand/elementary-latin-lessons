# Module 11 Quick Check — Months & the Calendar

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS NH.7.1, NH.8.2

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade)
**Title:** Module 11 Quick Check: Month Match
**Instructions shown to student:** Play the month-matching game below. Match each Latin month name to its season picture. Play as many times as you like — there's no wrong way to practice!
**Activity description:** A picture-matching activity with three tile pairs, mirroring the Part 3 widget from the Foundations lesson: (1) Iānuārius paired with a snowflake icon, (2) Iūlius paired with a sun icon, (3) Octōber paired with a pumpkin icon. Present the three Latin month names as word/audio tiles on one side and the three season icons as picture tiles on the other side; student taps or drags to pair them. Each correct pair gives a short spoken/on-screen "Yes!" confirmation; incorrect pairs simply invite another try (no penalty, no score shown). No independent reading is required — month names should be paired with audio playback of the word.
**Submission / completion criteria:** Mark complete when the student has paired all three tiles correctly at least once during a single sitting. No score or attempt count is recorded — this is a completion-only check.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions)
**Type:** Canvas Quiz, multiple choice, auto-graded
**Title:** Module 11 Quick Check: The Roman Calendar
**Instructions shown to student:** Answer these 5 questions about Latin month names and the Roman calendar puzzle. You may retake this quiz — your best score counts.

1. Which Latin word means "seven" and is the root of the month September?
   A. octō
   B. septem
   C. novem
   D. decem
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — septem means seven, and it's the root of September."

2. According to Roman tradition, how many months did the earliest Roman calendar have?
   A. 8
   B. 10
   C. 12
   D. 13
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — the earliest Roman calendar traditionally had only 10 months."

3. According to Roman tradition, which month did the earliest Roman calendar begin with?
   A. Iānuārius
   B. Mārtius
   C. September
   D. December
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — the year traditionally began in Mārtius (March), named for the god Mars."

4. Which Latin number-root means "ten" and gives December its name?
   A. septem
   B. octō
   C. novem
   D. decem
   **Correct: D** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — decem means ten, and it's the root of December."

5. Why does October's name come from octō (eight) even though it's our tenth month today?
   A. Because Rome originally started its year in March with only 10 months, so October really was the 8th month back then.
   B. Because the ancient Romans made a counting mistake and never fixed it.
   C. Because "October" always had 10 letters in it.
   D. Because octō secretly means "ten" in very old Latin.
   **Correct: A** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Exactly — when two months were added at the start of the year, the old number-names for September through December just stuck."
