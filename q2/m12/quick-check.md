# Module 12 Quick Check — Roads & Travel

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS NM.7.1, NH.7.2

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade)
**Title:** Module 12 Quick Check: Road Words Match
**Instructions shown to student:** Play the road-word matching game below. Match each Latin word to its picture. Play as many times as you like — there's no wrong way to practice!
**Activity description:** A picture-matching activity mirroring the Part 3 widget from the Foundations lesson: (1) via paired with a picture of a straight stone road, (2) iter paired with a picture of a packed traveling bag. Optionally add a third warm-up piece: a simple road-network graphic (a red Rome dot connected by striped "road" lines to two or three gold city dots) that the student can trace with a finger or mouse before doing the word match — this is a hands-on, untimed warm-up, not a scored step. Present the two Latin words as word/audio tiles on one side and the two pictures as tiles on the other; student taps or drags to pair them. Each correct pair gives a short spoken/on-screen "Yes!" confirmation; incorrect pairs simply invite another try (no penalty, no score shown). No independent reading is required — words should be paired with audio playback.
**Submission / completion criteria:** Mark complete when the student has paired both tiles correctly at least once during a single sitting. No score or attempt count is recorded — this is a completion-only check.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions)
**Type:** Canvas Quiz, multiple choice, auto-graded
**Title:** Module 12 Quick Check: Roman Roads
**Instructions shown to student:** Answer these 5 questions about Roman roads and today's Latin vocabulary. You may retake this quiz — your best score counts.

1. What does the Latin word via mean?
   A. journey, trip
   B. road, way
   C. bridge
   D. milestone
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — via means road or way."

2. What does the Latin word iter mean?
   A. journey, trip
   B. road, way
   C. stone
   D. map
   **Correct: A** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — iter means journey or trip."

3. Which English word grew out of the Latin word via?
   A. television
   B. avenue
   C. viaduct
   D. transit
   **Correct: C** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — viaduct comes from via, since a viaduct is a bridge that carries a road."

4. Which English word grew out of the Latin word iter?
   A. interior
   B. itinerary
   C. internet
   D. interval
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — itinerary comes from iter, since an itinerary is a plan for a journey."

5. What was the Via Appia?
   A. One of the oldest and most famous Roman roads, begun in 312 BC.
   B. A Roman aqueduct that carried water into the city.
   C. A type of Roman ship used for long sea voyages.
   D. The Roman name for a large open market.
   **Correct: A** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Exactly — the Via Appia (the Appian Way) was begun in 312 BC and is one of Rome's oldest, most famous roads."
