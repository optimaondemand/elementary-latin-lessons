📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
The Romans built roads that went on forever!
The Romans built long, straight stone roads that connected their whole empire. You may have heard people say "all roads lead to Rome" — that's because so many roads really did! Today we learn two Latin words about roads and trips.
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say via (road) and iter (trip).
2
I can point to a straight Roman road in a picture.
3
I can draw a road and show a grown-up one Latin word I learned.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED
▶
Headphones or speakers
▶
Paper and a crayon for drawing (a grown-up nearby is helpful but not required)

👣 PART 3 · ROAD WORDS

Trace the road with your finger from one city to Rome — then say these two words out loud with me!

🛣️
Via
it means "road"
🎒
Iter
it means "trip" or "journey"

Now you try: which word matches which picture?

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THE WORD
Tap the word that matches the picture.
🛣️ (a straight stone road)
🎒 (a packed traveling bag)
🤗 NEED HELP?
Two new words is plenty for one day! Scroll back up and look at the road picture again. Say via and iter out loud a few times before you pick.
▶  Go Further: trace a road on a real map with a grown-up
If you have a map at home, ask a grown-up to help you trace a road from your town to a city far away. That's your own iter!
