# Module 12 Reflection — Roads & Travel

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken/Drawn Reflection
**Type:** Canvas Assignment (facilitator-noted, completion-based)
**Prompt (read aloud to student):** "Draw a road that leads to a place you like to go, and tell a grown-up one Latin word you learned about roads today — via or iter."
**How it's captured:** A parent or facilitator jots a one-line note in the Canvas assignment comment (or checks a completion box) confirming the student drew a road and attempted to say one of the two Latin words aloud. No correctness judgment is needed — any sincere attempt counts as complete. The drawing itself can be photographed and attached if the family wishes, but this is optional.

## Primary (3-5) — Typed Reflection
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, text entry, completion or light credit
**Prompt:** "In one or two sentences, describe how Roman roads helped connect the empire, and use the word via or iter in your answer."
**Expected length:** 1-2 sentences
