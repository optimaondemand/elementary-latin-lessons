📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Counting past ten, Roman style!
You already know some Roman numerals. Today we go bigger — all the way up to twenty! We'll also meet two brand-new numeral shapes that stand for even bigger numbers. And we'll go spotting: Roman numerals are still hiding in the real world today, even on TV!
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say a few Latin number words past ten, like ūndecim (11) and vīgintī (20).
2
I can match a Roman numeral shape between XI and XX to how many it stands for.
3
I can point out a Roman numeral I spot in real life, like on a king or queen's name.

🤗 NEED HELP?
Counting dots one by one is the best trick. If a numeral looks like a jumble of letters, break it into little pieces: X is ten, so XI is just ten-plus-one.
▶  Go Further: hunt for a numeral at home
With a grown-up, look for a Roman numeral around your house — maybe on a clock face, a book, or a movie. How many can you find?
