# Module 09 Quick Check — Roman Numbers II (XI–XX, L, C)

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS NM.3.5, NH.7.1

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade)
**Title:** Module 9 Quick Check: Match the Numeral
**Instructions shown to student:** Play the numeral matching game below. Count the dots, then tap the Roman numeral that matches. Play as many times as you like!
**Activity description:** A picture-based matching game with 4 rounds. Each round shows a dot-picture (dots grouped in fives for easy counting, e.g. "⚫⚫⚫⚫⚫ ⚫⚫⚫⚫⚫ ⚫⚫" for 12) alongside two or three large Roman-numeral buttons (e.g. X, XI, XII, XX). The student taps the numeral that matches the dot count. Suggested rounds: 11 dots → XI vs. XX; 15 dots → X vs. XV; 18 dots → XV vs. XVIII; 20 dots → XI vs. XX. A bonus non-scored round can show the L and C shapes next to a crown icon and an Olympic-rings icon with the caption "these numerals are still used today — can you guess why?" for open talk with a grown-up, not graded. No independent reading is required; all quantities are shown as dot pictures, not digits or number words alone.
**Submission / completion criteria:** Marked complete when the student has played through all matching rounds once (correctness is not scored). Teacher may also accept a parent/facilitator note confirming the game was played together.
