# Module 09 Reflection — Roman Numbers II (XI–XX, L, C)

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken/Drawn Reflection
**Type:** Canvas Assignment (facilitator-noted, completion-based)
**Prompt (read aloud to student):** "Show a grown-up a Roman numeral between XI and XX. Can you tell them what number it stands for? Or draw your own numeral and the matching number of dots."
**How it's captured:** A parent or facilitator listens to the student name the numeral and its value (or looks at the student's drawing), then marks the assignment complete in Canvas with a one-line note such as "Student showed XV and correctly said fifteen" or "Student drew XII with 12 dots." No recording or written response is required from the student.

## Primary (3-5) — Typed Reflection
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, text entry, completion or light credit
**Prompt:** "Where have you seen a Roman numeral in real life — on a clock, in a book, in a king's or queen's name, or somewhere else? Write 1-2 sentences."
**Expected length:** 1-2 sentences
