# Module 10 Quick Check — Days of the Week

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS NM.3.5, NH.8.2

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade)
**Title:** Module 10 Quick Check: Match the Day
**Instructions shown to student:** Play the day-matching game below. Look at the picture, then tap the Latin day name that matches. Play as many times as you like!
**Activity description:** A picture-based matching game with 3 core rounds pairing a sky picture to its Latin day name: ☀️ (sun) → dies Sōlis; 🌙 (moon) → dies Lūnae; 🪐 (ringed planet) → dies Saturnī. Each round shows one picture and three Latin-word button choices. A bonus, non-scored round can introduce the remaining four sky pictures (a shield for Mars, a scroll for Mercury, a lightning bolt for Jupiter, a heart for Venus) paired loosely with "these days were also named for Roman gods" for open talk with a grown-up, without requiring recall. No independent reading is required; every round is picture-led.
**Submission / completion criteria:** Marked complete when the student has played through the three core matching rounds once (correctness is not scored). Teacher may also accept a parent/facilitator note confirming the game was played together.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions)
**Type:** Canvas Quiz, multiple choice, auto-graded
**Title:** Module 10 Quick Check: The Latin Week
**Instructions shown to student:** Answer each question about this module's Latin day names and the Roman-versus-Germanic day-name puzzle. You may retake this quiz for a better score.

1. Which Latin day name means "Sun's day"?
   A. dies Lūnae
   B. dies Sōlis
   C. dies Mārtis
   D. dies Saturnī
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — dies Sōlis is Sun's day, which became our Sunday."

2. Which English day comes from dies Lūnae?
   A. Sunday
   B. Monday
   C. Saturday
   D. Tuesday
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — dies Lūnae, Moon's day, became Monday."

3. Which English day still keeps its Roman god name directly, unlike Tuesday through Friday?
   A. Wednesday
   B. Thursday
   C. Saturday
   D. Friday
   **Correct: C** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — Saturday still sounds like dies Saturnī, Saturn's day."

4. The English name "Tuesday" comes from a Germanic war-god named Tiw. Which Roman god did English speakers think Tiw matched?
   A. Jupiter
   B. Mars
   C. Mercury
   D. Venus
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Exactly — Tiw was seen as matching Mars, the Roman war-god, even though the English name changed."

5. The English word "solar" comes from the Latin word for what?
   A. moon
   B. star
   C. sun
   D. earth
   **Correct: C** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — solar comes from sōl, the Latin word for sun."
