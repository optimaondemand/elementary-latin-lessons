📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Every day of your week has a Roman name hiding inside it!
Long ago, Romans named each day of the week after something in the sky — the sun, the moon, or one of a few wandering stars they connected to their gods. Some of our English day names still sound just like the Latin ones! Today we learn three of them: Sunday, Monday, and Saturday.
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say dies Sōlis (Sunday), dies Lūnae (Monday), and dies Saturnī (Saturday).
2
I can match a Latin day name to its picture: sun, moon, or ringed planet.
3
I can tell a grown-up which day is Sun's day on the calendar.

🌞 PART 2 · DAYS NAMED FOR THE SKY

Romans looked up at the sky to name their days. Say each Latin day name out loud with me!

☀️
dies Sōlis
Sunday — the Sun's day
🌙
dies Lūnae
Monday — the Moon's day
🪐
dies Saturnī
Saturday — Saturn's day

The other four days of the week were named for Roman gods too — Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, and Venus. Their English names changed a lot over time, so we'll save those for another day!
