# Module 10 Reflection — Days of the Week

## Foundations (K-2) — Spoken/Drawn Reflection
**Type:** Canvas Assignment (facilitator-noted, completion-based)
**Prompt (read aloud to student):** "Tell a grown-up: which day is dies Sōlis (Sun's day) on our calendar? Or draw a picture of the sun, moon, or ringed planet for your favorite of the three days we learned."
**How it's captured:** A parent or facilitator listens to the student name the correct day (or looks at the student's drawing), then marks the assignment complete in Canvas with a one-line note such as "Student correctly said dies Sōlis is Sunday" or "Student drew the moon for dies Lūnae." No recording or written response is required from the student.

## Primary (3-5) — Typed Reflection
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, text entry, completion or light credit
**Prompt:** "Which day-name story surprised you more: one of the Roman-named days (Sunday, Monday, Saturday) or one of the Germanic-named days (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday)? Explain in 1-2 sentences."
**Expected length:** 1-2 sentences
