🐕 PART 2 · READING: ANIMALS OF ROME
Romans lived alongside many of the same animal we know today. The canis (dog) guarded homes and farms. Archaeologists digging in the buried city of Pompeii found a floor mosaic at a house entrance that reads "Cave Canem" — "Beware of the Dog" — proof that Romans warned visitors about their guard dogs, just as a sign might today. The fēlēs (cat) was valued for keeping mice and rats away from stored grain. The equus (horse) carried messengers, pulled carts, and raced at places like the Circus Maximus.
