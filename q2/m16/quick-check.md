# Module 16 Quick Check — Animals of Rome

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS NM.8.1, NH.8.1

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade)
**Title:** Match the Animal
**Instructions shown to student:** Tap or point to the picture that matches each Latin animal word. Play as many times as you like — there's no clock and no wrong way to practice!
**Activity description:** A picture-matching activity (built as a Canvas Assignment with an embedded interactive, or a facilitator-led matching activity using printed/on-screen picture cards) presenting the four core words from this module — <em>animal</em> (any animal), <em>canis</em> (dog), <em>fēlēs</em> (cat), and <em>equus</em> (horse) — one at a time, each with 3 picture choices (1 correct, 2 distractors drawn from the other animal pictures so no answer is guessable by elimination alone). No reading of Latin text is required; the word may be spoken aloud by a narrator/audio cue alongside the picture choices. Untimed. A teacher building this in Canvas can either embed the lesson's `.ow-select` picture-matching widget or use a simple drag-and-drop/matching Canvas Assignment template.
**Submission / completion criteria:** Marked complete when the student has attempted a match for all four animal words, regardless of number of tries. No score is recorded beyond completion.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions)
**Type:** Canvas Quiz, multiple choice, auto-graded
**Title:** Module 16 Quick Check: Animals of Rome
**Instructions shown to student:** Answer each question about this module's Roman animal words, their meanings, and their English derivatives. You get 2 attempts; your best score counts.

1. What does the Latin word "canis" mean?
   A. cat
   B. horse
   C. dog
   D. sheep
   **Correct: C** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — canis means dog."

2. Which English word grew out of the Latin word "fēlēs" (cat)?
   A. feline
   B. canine
   C. equestrian
   D. bovine
   **Correct: A** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — fēlēs is the root of feline."

3. The word "Cave Canem," found on a mosaic in Pompeii, means:
   A. Welcome, friend
   B. Beware of the Dog
   C. Home of the Horse
   D. Feed the Cat
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — Cave Canem means Beware of the Dog, a warning sign at a Roman doorway."

4. Which English word comes from the Latin word "equus" (horse)?
   A. equestrian
   B. canine
   C. feline
   D. ovine
   **Correct: A** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — equus is the root of equestrian, a word about horseback riding."

5. In Roman households, cats were especially valued for:
   A. pulling carts
   B. guarding the front door
   C. keeping mice and rats away from stored grain
   D. carrying messages between cities
   **Correct: C** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — Romans valued cats for protecting stored grain from mice and rats."
