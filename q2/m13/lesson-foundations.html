📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
How did a Roman city get water without any pipes or pumps?
Romans built long stone paths called aqueducts to carry water from a faraway spring all the way into a city. The water didn't need a pump — it just gently rolled downhill the whole way! Today we follow the water on its long journey.

🌉 PART 2 · A BRIDGE FOR WATER

Water always rolls downhill. So Romans built a long stone path that sloped down, just a tiny bit, all the way from a spring far away into the city. When the ground dipped low, they built tall stone arches to carry the water path across, like a bridge just for water!

💧 The teal line on top is the water path, held up high by stone arches!

▶  Go Further: watch water flow downhill at home
