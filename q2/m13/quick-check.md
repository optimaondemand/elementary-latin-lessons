# Module 13 Quick Check — Aqueducts & Roman Engineering

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS NM.7.1, NH.7.2

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade)
**Title:** Module 13 Quick Check: Water's Journey
**Instructions shown to student:** "Play the matching game to put the water's journey in order. Play as many times as you like!"
**Activity description:** A picture-sequencing activity with four picture cards: a spring/lake (💧), a water channel, an arched stone bridge crossing a valley (🌉), and a city fountain (⛲). The student taps or drags the four pictures into the correct order, showing how water travels downhill in stages from its source to the city. Each picture has a short audio label (spoken word, e.g., "spring," "channel," "arches," "fountain") so the activity does not require reading. Placing a card correctly gives an immediate visual cue (a checkmark or gentle highlight); placing it incorrectly gives a gentle "try again" cue rather than a graded score. The activity is untimed and can be replayed without limit.
**Submission / completion criteria:** Marked complete once the student has attempted the full sequencing activity one time, regardless of whether every card was placed correctly. No correctness grade is recorded.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions)
**Type:** Canvas Quiz, multiple choice, auto-graded
**Title:** Module 13 Quick Check: Aqueducts & Roman Engineering
**Instructions shown to student:** "Answer these 5 questions about today's lesson on Roman aqueducts. You have 2 attempts — your highest score will be recorded."

1. What is an aqueduct built to do?
   A. Store grain for the winter
   B. Carry water across long distances using gravity
   C. Defend a city from attackers
   D. Pump water uphill using machines
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Right — aqueducts use a gentle downhill slope to let gravity carry water for miles."

2. What Latin word means "water"?
   A. aquaeductus
   B. aqua
   C. aquila
   D. via
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — aqua means water, and it's the root of aqueduct."

3. Why did Romans build tall stone arches (an arcade) in some places along an aqueduct?
   A. To make the aqueduct look decorative only
   B. To carry the water channel across a low valley and keep the slope steady
   C. To store extra water for a drought
   D. To block enemy soldiers from the city
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Correct — arches let the channel cross valleys without losing its steady downhill slope."

4. The Pont du Gard, a famous surviving Roman aqueduct bridge, can still be seen today in which country?
   A. Italy
   B. Greece
   C. France
   D. Spain
   **Correct: C** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "That's right — the Pont du Gard stands in southern France."

5. Which English word grew out of the Latin word aqua?
   A. audio
   B. aquarium
   C. agriculture
   D. arcade
   **Correct: B** — Feedback shown for correct answer: "Yes — aquarium comes from aqua, since it's a water tank."
