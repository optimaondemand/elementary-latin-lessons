📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Romans didn't wear the same thing every day!
Just like you might wear something special for a birthday party, Romans had clothes just for regular days and clothes just for big, special days. Today we find out what those clothes looked like — and learn two new Latin words for them.

👕 PART 2 · WHAT ROMANS WORE

Almost every Roman — moms, dads, and kids — wore simple clothes every single day. But on really big, formal days, some grown-ups put on one giant extra piece of cloth over their everyday clothes. Here are three things to know.

👕 Did you know?Almost everyone — men, women, and children — wore a simple tunic called a tunica every day.
🏛️ Did you know?The toga was a large piece of cloth wrapped around the body. It was worn on special, formal days.
🧑 Did you know?Mostly grown-up men who were Roman citizens wore the toga. It showed that they belonged to Rome.
🎽 PART 3 · TWO NEW LATIN WORDS

Here are the two Latin words for what Romans wore. Say them out loud with me!

Tunica
Simple, everyday clothes — almost everyone wore one!
Toga
A big, draped outfit worn OVER a tunica, only for special days!

Now you try: dress the Roman! Is this outfit for an everyday moment, or a special day?
