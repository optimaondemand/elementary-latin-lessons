## Primary Lane (Grades 3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Format:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, 5 multiple-choice questions, 4 answer options each.

**Question 1**
What was a tunica?
A) A large draped cloth worn only by senators
B) Simple, everyday clothing worn by nearly everyone — men, women, and children *(correct)*
C) A type of Roman sandal
D) A ceremonial crown worn by emperors

Feedback (correct): "Right! The tunica was simple, everyday clothing worn by nearly everyone in Rome."

**Question 2**
What was a toga?
A) An undergarment worn beneath the tunica by all Romans
B) A children's play costume
C) A large draped woolen garment worn over a tunica on formal occasions *(correct)*
D) A cloak worn only by soldiers in battle

Feedback (correct): "Correct! The toga was a large draped woolen garment worn over the tunica for formal, public occasions."

**Question 3**
Who typically wore the toga?
A) Adult male Roman citizens *(correct)*
B) Everyone in Rome, every day
C) Only children on their birthdays
D) Only enslaved workers

Feedback (correct): "Yes! The toga was specifically associated with adult male Roman citizens — it was a mark of citizenship and status, not everyday wear."

**Question 4**
Which of these was true of the tunica but NOT the toga?
A) It was worn draped over other clothing
B) It was reserved for formal occasions only
C) It was worn by people of varying wealth, in finer or plainer versions *(correct)*
D) It was worn only by citizens

Feedback (correct): "Correct! The tunica was everyday clothing worn by people of all kinds — its quality just varied with how wealthy the family was."

**Question 5**
The English word "tunic" comes from which Latin word?
A) toga
B) terra
C) tunica *(correct)*
D) porta

Feedback (correct): "Yes! The English word 'tunic' comes directly from the Latin tunica."
