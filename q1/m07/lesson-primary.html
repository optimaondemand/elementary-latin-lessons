📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Not every Roman wore the same thing.
Most Romans wore simple, everyday clothing. But one particular outfit was reserved for a very specific group of people, on very specific occasions. Today you'll learn what Romans actually wore, who wore what, and why — and you'll meet an English word that comes straight from Latin.

📜 PART 2 · READING: TWO KINDS OF ROMAN DRESS
Most Romans, rich or poor, young or old, wore a simple piece of clothing called a tunica nearly every day. A wealthy family's tunica might be made of finer cloth, while a poorer family's was plain, but almost everyone from small children to grandparents wore one. Only on formal, important occasions did an adult male Roman citizen put on a toga, a large piece of woolen cloth wrapped and draped over the tunica. Women and children did not typically wear the toga — it marked a man as a grown, adult citizen, not just everyday clothing.
Tunica
Everyday clothing, worn by nearly everyone, of finer or plainer quality depending on wealth.
Toga
Draped over the tunica, worn only for formal occasions by adult male citizens.
