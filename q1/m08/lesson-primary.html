📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why did the Romans have so many gods?
The ancient Romans honored many gods, and they believed each god had a domain: a special part of the world or of life that god watched over. Today you meet three of the most important Roman gods and learn what each one was known for.

🏛️ PART 2 · READING: DOMAINS OF THE GODS
The ancient Romans honored many gods, and each one had a special domain, a part of the world they watched over. Iuppiter (Jupiter) was king of the gods and ruled over the sky, sending thunder and lightning; his symbols were the eagle and the thunderbolt. Iūnō (Juno), queen of the gods, watched over marriage and family and was honored as a protector of Rome itself; her symbol was the peacock. Neptūnus (Neptune) ruled the sea, and sailors recognized him by his three-pronged spear, the trident.
