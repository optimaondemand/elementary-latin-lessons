# Quarter 1 Roman Passport — Page 1 (Module 08 Milestone)

> **Teacher note (not shown to students):** This is the Quarter 1 summative milestone. It replaces the usual Module 08 Quick Check — see quick-check.md. No numeric grade beyond completion; this is a celebrated running project, not a test.

## Foundations Lane (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Student-facing instructions:**
It's Roman Passport time! Draw a picture of something you learned about this quarter — it could be Rome or Italy, a Roman numeral, the story of the she-wolf, a Roman family or home, Roman food or clothing, or one of the gods you just met (Iuppiter, Iūnō, or Neptūnus). When your picture is done, tell a grown-up one fact about it. Your grown-up will write your fact down under your picture — that's your very first Roman Passport page!

**Facilitator-facing instructions:**
Give the student a blank sheet of paper or a page in their Roman Passport document. Let the student choose freely from anything covered this quarter (Modules 1–8) — do not steer them toward a "correct" answer. After the student draws, ask them to say one fact about their picture out loud, and write that fact underneath the drawing exactly as the student states it (light spelling/grammar cleanup is fine; preserve the student's own words and idea). Photograph or scan the finished page and upload it to Canvas as the Module 08 submission, or paste it directly into the student's Roman Passport document if working digitally. Completion is the only requirement — there is no right or wrong answer, and no numeric grade.

## Primary Lane (Grades 3-5) — Canvas Assignment (Roman Passport Word document)

**Instructions:**
Open your Roman Passport Word document. Go to **Page 1** — this is the page you'll return to and add a new entry every quarter this year, so it becomes a running record of everything you learn.

For this quarter's entry:
1. Draw a picture directly on the page, or find and paste in an appropriate picture, of something you learned about this quarter (Rome or Italy, Roman numerals, the she-wolf story, a Roman family or home, food or clothing, or one of today's gods).
2. Underneath the picture, write **one fact** you learned this quarter in your own words.
3. Below that, write **one Latin word** you learned this quarter and its meaning (for example, one of today's names — Iuppiter, Iūnō, or Neptūnus — or a word from an earlier module).

Save your document and submit it to Canvas. Next quarter, you'll come back to this same Roman Passport document and add a new entry on the next page, building your passport one quarter at a time all year.
