# Module 02 Quick Check — The City of Rome

> **Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS codes — NM.7.2, NH.7.2

## Foundations Lane (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Format:** Canvas Assignment, completion grade only (no numeric score).

**Student-facing instructions:**
"Match each picture to the Latin word it goes with. Draw a line to connect them, or tell a grown-up which ones go together out loud. There's no wrong way to try — this is just for practice!"

Present four picture cards and four word cards (mixed order) for students to pair up:

| Picture card | Latin word | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 🏛️ a city drawing with hills behind it | **Rōma** | Rome, the city |
| 👢 a boot-shaped map | **Italia** | Italy, the boot-shaped land |
| ⛰️ a hill or small mountain | **mōns** | a hill (or mountain) |
| 🏝️ an island sitting in water | **īnsula** | an island |

**Teacher answer key (matching pairs):** city → Rōma; boot map → Italia; hill → mōns; island → īnsula.

**Grading:** Mark complete for any good-faith attempt at all four pairs; no pair needs to be "correct" for credit. If a facilitator notes a student consistently reverses one pair, that's useful information for Module 3 review, not a grading concern here.

## Primary Lane (Grades 3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Format:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, best of 2 attempts, 5 multiple-choice questions.

**Question 1**
What does the Latin word *Rōma* refer to?
A) A mountain range
B) The city built up across seven hills *(correct)*
C) A river in Egypt
D) A Latin verb
Feedback (correct): "Correct! Rōma is the city built up across seven hills."

**Question 2**
Which Latin word means "island"?
A) mōns
B) Italia
C) īnsula *(correct)*
D) Rōma
Feedback (correct): "Right! Īnsula is the Latin word for island."

**Question 3**
What river ran through the growing city of Rome?
A) The Nile
B) The Thames
C) The Tiber *(correct)*
D) The Seine
Feedback (correct): "Yes! The Tiber River carried people and goods into the growing city."

**Question 4**
The English word *peninsula* comes from combining *paene* ("almost") with which Latin word?
A) mōns
B) īnsula *(correct)*
C) Rōma
D) Italia
Feedback (correct): "Exactly! Peninsula = paene ('almost') + īnsula ('island') — 'almost an island.'"

**Question 5**
Italia is shaped like a...
A) circle
B) tall boot *(correct)*
C) square
D) triangle
Feedback (correct): "Correct! Italia is shaped like a tall boot reaching into the Mediterranean Sea."
