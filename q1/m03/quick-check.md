# Module 03 Quick Check — Roman Numbers I (I-X)

> **Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS codes — NM.3.5, NH.7.1

## Foundations Lane (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Assignment title:** Roman Numeral Sort

**Format:** Canvas Assignment, completion grade only — no numeric grade. Submission type: on-paper or on-screen sort, with a photo, screen-share, or verbal check-off from a parent/facilitator as the "submission."

**Student-facing instructions:**
"You will sort ten cards. Each card shows a number of dots (like ●●●) next to a symbol. Some symbols are Roman numerals (like III) and some are plain numbers (like 3). Sort the cards into two piles: 'Roman numeral' and 'regular number.' Then, for each Roman numeral card, say the Latin number word out loud — ūnus, duo, trēs, quattuor, quīnque, sex, septem, octō, novem, decem. When you're done, show your two piles to a grown-up."

**Materials:** 10 printable or teacher-provided cards showing a dot quantity paired with either a Roman numeral or an Arabic digit (teacher may print two sets — one all-Roman, one all-Arabic — and mix them, or provide as a digital drag-sort in Canvas).

**Matching-pairs / sort key (for teacher use):**

| Dot quantity | Roman numeral card | Arabic digit card | Latin word |
|---|---|---|---|
| ● | I | 1 | ūnus |
| ●● | II | 2 | duo |
| ●●● | III | 3 | trēs |
| ●●●● | IV | 4 | quattuor |
| ●●●●● | V | 5 | quīnque |
| ●●●●●● | VI | 6 | sex |
| ●●●●●●● | VII | 7 | septem |
| ●●●●●●●● | VIII | 8 | octō |
| ●●●●●●●●● | IX | 9 | novem |
| ●●●●●●●●●● | X | 10 | decem |

**Completion criteria:** Student sorts all 10 cards into the correct pile (Roman numeral vs. regular number) and attempts to say at least a few of the Latin number words out loud. No score is recorded beyond complete/incomplete.

## Primary Lane (Grades 3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Quiz title:** Module 3 Quick Check — Roman Numbers I (I-X)
**Settings:** Auto-graded, best of 2 attempts, 5 multiple choice questions, 4 options each.

**Question 1**
What is the Latin word for the number "one"?
- A) duo
- B) **ūnus** ✅ (correct)
- C) decem
- D) trēs

*Feedback shown for correct answer:* "Right! Ūnus means one."

**Question 2**
Which Latin word means "ten"?
- A) novem
- B) **decem** ✅ (correct)
- C) octō
- D) sex

*Feedback shown for correct answer:* "Yes! Decem is the Latin word for ten (10)."

**Question 3**
What quantity does the Roman numeral "VII" represent?
- A) 5
- B) 6
- C) **7** ✅ (correct)
- D) 9

*Feedback shown for correct answer:* "Right! VII stands for seven (septem)."

**Question 4**
Many analog clocks display their hours using Roman numerals. On some of these clocks, which numeral is written differently than you might expect?
- A) **Four is written IIII instead of IV** ✅ (correct)
- B) Ten is written XV instead of X
- C) One is written II instead of I
- D) Six is written IX instead of VI

*Feedback shown for correct answer:* "Correct! Some clockmakers write four as IIII instead of IV — it's a real, old tradition that has stuck around for centuries."

**Question 5**
The NFL gives each Super Bowl its own Roman numeral instead of a regular number. Which of these is an example of how a Super Bowl might be labeled?
- A) Super Bowl 58
- B) **Super Bowl LVIII** ✅ (correct)
- C) Super Bowl VIIII
- D) Super Bowl Fifty-Eight

*Feedback shown for correct answer:* "Yes! The NFL uses Roman numerals, like LVIII, instead of a plain number."
