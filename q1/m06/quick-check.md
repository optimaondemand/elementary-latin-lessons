# Module 06 Quick Check — Roman Food & Market

> **Teacher note (not shown to students):** CPALMS codes — NM.6.4, NH.6.3

## Foundations Lane (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Student-facing instructions:**
"Look at the pictures below. Say or tap the Latin word that matches each food picture. Then sort the food pictures into two groups: **Roman Menu** and **Not Roman**. Play as many times as you like — this is just for fun, and there is no grade for getting it 'right'!"

**Matching pairs (Latin word ↔ picture) — teacher key:**
- 🍞 loaf of bread ↔ *pānis* (bread)
- 🍽️ plate with several foods ↔ *cibus* (food)
- 🛒 market stall / outdoor stand ↔ *mercātus* (market)

**Sort key — Roman Menu vs. Not Roman:**
- Roman Menu: 🍞 bread, 🫒 olives, 🧀 cheese, 🥬 vegetables (and, as an occasional special-occasion item, 🍗 meat or fish)
- Not Roman: 🍔 hamburger, 🍕 pizza slice

**Grading:** Completion only. Mark complete once the student has attempted the matching and sorting activity; no numeric score is recorded.

## Primary Lane (Grades 3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Question 1**
What does the Latin word *cibus* mean?
- A) Market
- B) **Food** ✅ (correct)
- C) Bread
- D) Harvest
*Feedback (correct):* "Correct! Cibus is the general Latin word for food."

**Question 2**
Which Latin word means "bread" — a staple eaten at nearly every Roman meal?
- A) cibus
- B) **pānis** ✅ (correct)
- C) mercātus
- D) Ceres
*Feedback (correct):* "Right! Pānis means bread, and it was a staple of the Roman diet."

**Question 3**
What was the *mercātus*?
- A) A Roman bathhouse
- B) A Roman temple
- C) **A Roman marketplace, often located near the Forum** ✅ (correct)
- D) A Roman school
*Feedback (correct):* "Yes! The mercātus was the outdoor marketplace where Romans bought their food."

**Question 4**
The English word "mercantile" comes from which Latin word?
- A) pānis
- B) cibus
- C) **mercātus** ✅ (correct)
- D) Ceres
*Feedback (correct):* "Right! Mercantile comes from mercātus and mercārī, meaning 'to trade.'"

**Question 5**
The English word "cereal" comes from which source?
- A) The Latin word *pānis* (bread)
- B) The Latin word *cibus* (food)
- C) **Ceres, the Roman goddess of the harvest and agriculture** ✅ (correct)
- D) The Latin word *mercātus* (market)
*Feedback (correct):* "Correct! Cereal comes from Ceres, the Roman goddess linked to grain and the harvest — not directly from pānis or cibus."

**Coverage note:** Q1-Q3 cover vocabulary and Roman food facts (the general word for food, bread as a daily staple, and the marketplace's location near the Forum); Q4 covers the mercantile cognate; Q5 covers the Ceres/cereal fact, correctly distinguishing it from pānis and cibus.
