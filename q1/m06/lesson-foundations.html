📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Bread, olives, and cheese — a peek at a Roman lunch!
Every day, Roman families ate bread, olives, and cheese. Today we learn the Latin word for food, the Latin word for bread, and where Romans went shopping for it!
🏛️ YOUR GUIDE · MAGISTER CAESAR
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say cibus (food) and pānis (bread).
2
I can point to pictures of foods Romans ate.
3
I can show a grown-up a picture of a Roman food.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED
▶

🍽️ PART 2 · WHAT DID ROMANS EAT?

Roman families ate simple foods almost every day. Look at the pictures below!

🍞
Bread
eaten almost every day
🫒
Olives
and olive oil, too
🧀
Cheese
a Roman favorite
🥬
Vegetables
grown near home

On special days, a Roman family might also eat meat or fish — but that was a treat, not an every-day food.

🗣️ PART 3 · TWO NEW WORDS

A Roman child used these two words about food. Say them out loud with me!

🍽️
Cibus
it means "food"
🍞
Pānis
it means "bread"

Now you try: which word matches which picture?

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THE WORD
Tap the word that matches the picture.
🍞 (a loaf of bread)
🍽️ (a plate with many different foods)
🛒 PART 4 · GOING TO THE MERCĀTUS

Romans did not have grocery stores like we do. Instead, they walked to the mercātus — a busy outdoor market, often near the middle of the city. That is where families bought their cibus!

🛒
Mercātus
it means "market" — a place to buy food

Now for the big game! Look at each picture. Was this food sold at a Roman mercātus, or is it a food from today that Romans never had?

⚡ BIG GAME · ROMAN MENU OR NOT?
Sort each food: is it Roman, or not?
🍞 Bread
🫒 Olives
🧀 Cheese
🍔 Hamburger
🍕 Pizza Slice
🤗 NEED HELP?
Take your time — there is no clock running! Look closely at each picture. If it is something a Roman family could have grown, baked, or made themselves, it belongs on the Roman Menu.
▶  Go Further: talk about food with a grown-up
Ask a grown-up what they ate for lunch today. Is any of it the same as what a Roman family ate — like bread, cheese, or vegetables?
