📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
What did ordinary Romans really eat, and where did they buy it?
Most Romans were not feasting on exotic delicacies. Their everyday diet centered on bread, olives and olive oil, cheese, and vegetables — with meat or fish saved for special occasions. To get their food, they walked to the mercātus, an open-air market usually set up near the Forum, the public square at the heart of the city. Today you'll learn the Latin words for food, bread, and market — and see how one of them lives on in English.

📜 PART 2 · READING: SHOPPING FOR SUPPER
Most Romans ate simple food each day. Pānis, or bread, was eaten at nearly every meal, along with olives, olive oil, cheese, and vegetables. Meat or fish appeared only on special occasions, since they cost more than everyday staples. To buy their cibus, Romans walked to the mercātus, an open-air market usually set up near the Forum, the busy public square at the heart of the city.
