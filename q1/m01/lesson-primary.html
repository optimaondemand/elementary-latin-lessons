📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why do we study a language nobody speaks at the grocery store?
Latin was the everyday language of ancient Rome. No country uses it as its everyday language anymore — but Latin never really disappeared. It's inside English words you already use, it's the language doctors and scientists still borrow from, and it's the root of Spanish, French, Italian, and Portuguese. This year, you'll start noticing it everywhere.

🗺️ PART 2 · READING: THE ETERNAL CITY
Rome sits in the country we now call Italia (Italy) — a peninsula shaped like a tall boot, reaching into the Mediterranean Sea. People have called Rome "the Eternal City" for over two thousand years, because no matter what changed around it, the city itself never stopped being important. When a Roman greeted a friend, they said salvē. When they said goodbye, they said valē. Simple words — and you can say them today exactly as a Roman child once did.

🔍 PART 3 · DERIVATIVE HUNT

A derivative is an English word that grew out of a Latin one. Match each Latin word below to the English word it helped create.

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THE DERIVATIVE
Which English word grew out of this Latin word?
1. grātus — "thankful, pleasing" (as in grātiās tibi agō, "thank you")
2. vīvere — "to live"
3. terra — "earth, land"
🤗 NEED HELP?
Derivative hunting takes practice — even adults miss these at first. If a match feels tricky, say the Latin word out loud a few times and listen for the English word buried inside it.
