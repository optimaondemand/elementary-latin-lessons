📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
What did a Roman family's house actually look like?
A well-off Roman family lived in a house called a domus, built around one central room open to the sky. Today you'll step inside that room, meet the words Romans used for the people in a familia, and picture a day in a Roman child's life.

🏛️ PART 2 · READING: INSIDE THE DOMUS
A well-off Roman family's house, called a domus, was built around one grand room called the atrium. The atrium had an opening in its roof called a compluvium, which let in daylight and open air. Rainwater falling through the compluvium collected in a shallow pool below called the impluvium. Smaller rooms opened onto the atrium from every side, so the whole family passed through this one shared center every day.

👪 PART 3 · A DAY IN A ROMAN CHILD'S LIFE

A Roman child's day changed as they grew. Younger children spent much of their time playing with simple toys. Around age six or seven, many boys, and some girls, began attending lessons for the first time. The pater, the father, stood as head of the household, while every member of the familia had a part to play at home.

🔤 FAMILY WORDS
familia — family
domus — house, home
māter — mother
pater — father
fīlius — son
fīlia — daughter
