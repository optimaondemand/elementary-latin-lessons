📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Step inside a Roman family's house!
A Roman family lived together in a house called a domus. Today we peek inside one and meet the family who lives there — and learn the Latin words for mother, father, son, and daughter.
🏛️ YOUR GUIDE · MAGISTER CAESAR
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say domus (house) and name the people in a familia: māter, pater, fīlius, fīlia.
2
I can point to the open room in the middle of a Roman house in a picture.
3
I can match Latin family words to the right picture in a family tree.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED
▶
Headphones or speakers
▶
A grown-up nearby is helpful but not required

🏛️ PART 2 · A HOUSE CALLED DOMUS

A well-off Roman family's house was called a domus. Right in the middle was one big room with a hole in the roof! Sunshine and rain came right through the hole, and the rain water collected in a little pool below.
