📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
One of Rome's own stories about its own beginning.
Ancient Romans told a legend — a traditional story, passed down over time — about how their city began. Historians today don't think it happened exactly this way, and there's no way to prove it. But the story mattered enormously to the Romans themselves, and it's still one of the most famous legends from the ancient world.

📜 PART 2 · READING: THE LEGEND OF ROMULUS AND REMUS
👶👶  🐺  🏛️
Twin baby brothers → found by a she-wolf → a new city
The Romans told a legend about twin baby brothers named Rōmulus and Remus, who were left by a river as infants. According to the story, a lupa (she-wolf) — a real animal that lives in the wild — found the twins and cared for them until they grew up. As young men, the two frātrēs (brothers) decided to build a new urbs (city) together, though the legend says they disagreed about exactly where to build it. In the end, Romulus became the city's first king and gave the new city his own name: Rōma.

This is one of Rome's own legends about its own beginnings — not verified history. Historians don't think the city was really founded exactly this way, but the story was hugely important to how Romans understood themselves. Watch this short video for a closer look at the legend.

🔍 PART 3 · WORD MATCH

Some Latin words from this legend are still hiding in English today. Match each Latin word to the English word it's connected to (or, for one word, to its meaning).

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THE WORD
Which English word is connected to this Latin word?
1. urbs — "city"
2. frāter — "brother"
3. lupa — what does this word mean?
🤗 NEED HELP?
Scroll back up to the reading if a word feels tricky. Urbs and frāter both grew into English words over time; lupa is just the Latin word for the animal in the story.
