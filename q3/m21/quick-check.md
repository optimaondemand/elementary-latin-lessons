# Module 21 Quick Check — Gods of Rome III

**Teacher-facing note (not shown to students):** CPALMS/Florida World Languages standards for this module — WL.K12.NM.6.4, WL.K12.NH.2.2.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade attached).

**Activity — "Match the God" picture match:** Students see four symbol pictures (a lyre/music note 🎵, a bow and arrow 🏹, a hammer 🔨, and a hearth fire 🔥) alongside the four new god names (*Apollō, Diāna, Vulcānus, Vesta*), presented as a picture-matching/sorting game (the same click-to-match widget style used in the lesson, or a teacher-led verbal version for a facilitator to run live). The student matches each symbol to its spoken god name. No text reading is required — the teacher or the audio narration says each name aloud, and the student points to or clicks the matching symbol. One review symbol (the thunderbolt ⚡ for Iuppiter) may be mixed in to keep the game cumulative.

**Directions shown to student/facilitator:** "Listen to each god's name. Point to (or click) the symbol that matches."

**Submission:** Facilitator marks the assignment complete once the student has matched all four new symbols, in one sitting or across a few tries. No minimum score.

**Grading:** Full credit for completion. No partial credit distinctions — this is a completion-based Canvas assignment, not an auto-graded quiz.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What is Apollō the god of?
A) The sea and storms
B) The sun, light, music, and prophecy
C) Fire and the forge
D) Wisdom and crafts
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — Apollō rules the sun, light, music, and the ability to see into the future."

**Q2.** What is Diāna's symbol?
A) A trident
B) A peacock
C) A bow and arrow
D) A hammer
**Correct: C**
Feedback (correct): "Yes — Diāna, goddess of the hunt and the moon, is shown with a bow and arrow."

**Q3.** Vulcānus is the god of which two things?
A) Fire and metalworking (the forge)
B) The hunt and the moon
C) Marriage and the sky
D) Love and beauty
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Correct — Vulcānus works at the forge as the god of fire and metalworking."

**Q4.** What does Vesta watch over?
A) The sea
B) The hearth fire and the home
C) The sun and prophecy
D) War
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Exactly — Vesta guards the hearth fire at the center of every Roman home."

**Q5.** This is your third round of Roman gods this year. Which symbol belongs to Iuppiter, a god you met back in Module 8?
A) An owl
B) A thunderbolt
C) A lyre
D) Winged sandals
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — Iuppiter, king of the gods, is known by his thunderbolt."
