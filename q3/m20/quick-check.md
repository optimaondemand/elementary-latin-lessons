# Module 20 Quick Check — Weather & Seasons

**Teacher-facing note (not shown to students):** CPALMS/Florida World Languages standards for this module — WL.K12.NM.3.5, WL.K12.NH.6.1.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade attached).

**Activity — "Match the Season" picture match:** Students see four season pictures (❄️ snow, 🌱 a green sprout, ☀️ a bright sun, 🍂 a falling leaf) alongside the four Latin words (*vēr, aestās, hiems, autumnus*), presented as a picture-matching/sorting game (the same click-to-match widget style used in the lesson, or a teacher-led verbal version for a facilitator to run live). The student matches each season picture to its spoken Latin word. No text reading is required — the teacher or the audio narration says each word aloud, and the student points to or clicks the matching picture.

**Directions shown to student/facilitator:** "Listen to each Latin season word. Point to (or click) the picture that matches."

**Submission:** Facilitator marks the assignment complete once the student has matched all four seasons, in one sitting or across a few tries. No minimum score.

**Grading:** Full credit for completion. No partial credit distinctions — this is a completion-based Canvas assignment, not an auto-graded quiz.

---

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What does the Latin word *vēr* mean?
A) Winter
B) Summer
C) Spring
D) Autumn
**Correct: C**
Feedback (correct): "Right — vēr means spring."

**Q2.** What does the Latin word *aestās* mean?
A) Summer
B) Winter
C) Spring
D) Autumn
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Yes — aestās means summer."

**Q3.** According to the lesson, why did Roman farmers pay such close attention to the four seasons?
A) To know when to plant and harvest their crops
B) To plan religious festivals only
C) To decide when to travel to Rome
D) To choose what clothes to wear
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Correct — Roman farmers organized planting and harvesting around the seasons."

**Q4.** What does the Latin word *hiems* mean?
A) Spring
B) Summer
C) Autumn
D) Winter
**Correct: D**
Feedback (correct): "Exactly — hiems means winter."

**Q5.** Which English word comes directly from the Latin word *autumnus*?
A) August
B) Autumn
C) Automatic
D) Author
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — the English word autumn comes directly from Latin autumnus."
