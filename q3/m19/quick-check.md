# Module 19 Quick Check — School in Rome

**Teacher-facing note (not shown to students):** CPALMS/Florida World Languages standards for this module — WL.K12.NM.6.1, WL.K12.NH.6.1.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade attached).

**Activity — "School Tools: Then and Now" picture match:** Students see three Roman school-tool pictures (wax tablet & stylus, abacus, scroll) alongside three modern school-tool pictures (notebook & pencil, calculator, book), presented as a picture-matching/sorting game (the same click-to-match widget style used in the lesson, or a teacher-led verbal version for a facilitator to run live). The student matches each Roman tool to the modern tool we use instead today. No text reading is required — the teacher or the audio narration names each tool aloud, and the student points to or clicks the matching picture.

**Directions shown to student/facilitator:** "Look at each Roman school tool. Point to (or click) the modern tool we use instead today."

**Submission:** Facilitator marks the assignment complete once the student has matched all three pairs, in one sitting or across a few tries. No minimum score.

**Grading:** Full credit for completion. No partial credit distinctions — this is a completion-based Canvas assignment, not an auto-graded quiz.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What does the Latin word *lūdus* mean?
A) Teacher
B) Student
C) School
D) Book
**Correct: C**
Feedback (correct): "Right — lūdus means school, the place a Roman discipulus went each day."

**Q2.** What does the Latin word *discipulus* mean?
A) Teacher
B) Student
C) Tool
D) Building
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Yes — discipulus means student."

**Q3.** What does the Latin word *magister* mean?
A) Student
B) School
C) Teacher
D) Scroll
**Correct: C**
Feedback (correct): "Correct — magister means teacher."

**Q4.** Roman students practiced writing on a wax tablet using a pointed tool. What was that tool called?
A) Stylus
B) Abacus
C) Scroll
D) Toga
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Exactly — a stylus was the pointed tool used to write on a wax tablet, and the wax could be smoothed flat again to reuse it."

**Q5.** Which English word grew out of the Latin word *magister* ("teacher")?
A) Magic
B) Magistrate
C) Magnet
D) Major
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — magistrate, a word for a judge or official, comes from magister."
