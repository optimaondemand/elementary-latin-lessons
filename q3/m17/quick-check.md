# Module 17 Quick Check — Colors in Latin

**Teacher-facing note (not shown to students):** CPALMS/Florida World Languages standards for this module — WL.K12.NM.3.5, WL.K12.NH.8.1.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade attached).

**Activity — "Build the Mosaic" picture match:** Students see four colored tiles (red, white, black, green) alongside the four Latin words (*ruber, albus, niger, viridis*), presented as a picture-matching/sorting game (the same click-to-match widget style used in the lesson, or a teacher-led verbal version for a facilitator to run live). The student matches each colored tile to its spoken Latin word. No text reading is required — the teacher or the audio narration says each word aloud, and the student points to or clicks the matching color.

**Directions shown to student/facilitator:** "Listen to each Latin color word. Point to (or click) the tile that matches."

**Submission:** Facilitator marks the assignment complete once the student has matched all four colors, in one sitting or across a few tries. No minimum score.

**Grading:** Full credit for completion. No partial credit distinctions — this is a completion-based Canvas assignment, not an auto-graded quiz.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What does the Latin word *ruber* mean?
A) Green
B) Red
C) Black
D) White
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — ruber means red, just like the red tiles in a mosaic."

**Q2.** What does the Latin word *viridis* mean?
A) White
B) Black
C) Green
D) Red
**Correct: C**
Feedback (correct): "Yes — viridis means green."

**Q3.** A Roman mosaic was built from small pieces of stone or glass. What were these small pieces called?
A) Tesserae
B) Ludi
C) Toga
D) Forum
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Correct — tesserae are the small colored pieces used to build a mosaic."

**Q4.** Which English word grew out of the Latin word *ruber* ("red")?
A) Rural
B) Rubric
C) Ribbon
D) Reptile
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Exactly — rubric comes from ruber, since red ink was once used to mark important rules or headings."

**Q5.** Which English word grew out of the Latin word *viridis* ("green")?
A) Verdant
B) Vibrant
C) Virtue
D) Visible
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Right — verdant, meaning lush and green, comes from viridis."
