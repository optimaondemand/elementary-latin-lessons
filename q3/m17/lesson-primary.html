📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why does "rubric" sound like a Roman color?
Romans decorated floors and walls with mosaics — pictures built from tiny pieces of colored stone and glass called tesserae. To build one, a Roman craftsman needed to know his colors well. Today you'll learn four Latin color words — and discover that two of them are still hiding inside English words you already use.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can use four Latin color words: ruber, albus, niger, viridis.
2

3
I can name two English words that grew out of Latin color words.

🎨 PART 2 · READING: PICTURES MADE OF STONE
A Roman mosaic was built one tiny piece at a time. Craftsmen called the small colored pieces tesserae, and they were cut from stone, tile, or glass. To plan a picture, a craftsman needed exact color words: ruber for red, albus for white, niger for black, and viridis for green. Some Roman mosaics have survived for almost two thousand years and can still be seen today, their colors barely faded.

🤗 NEED HELP?
Say each Latin color word slowly and listen for it buried in the English choices. Ruber sounds like the start of "rubric." Viridis sounds like the start of "verdant."
