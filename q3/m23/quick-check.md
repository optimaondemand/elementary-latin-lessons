# Module 23 Quick Check — Pompeii: A City Frozen in Time

**Teacher-facing note (not shown to students):** CPALMS/Florida World Languages standards for this module — WL.K12.NM.6.3, WL.K12.NH.6.3.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade attached).

**Activity — "Time Capsule Treasure" picture match:** Students see four pictures of things found preserved at Pompeii (a loaf of bread, a pottery jar, a piece of jewelry, a wall painting) alongside their names, presented as a picture-matching/sorting game (the same click-to-match widget style used in the lesson, or a teacher-led verbal version for a facilitator to run live). The student matches each picture to its spoken name. No text reading is required — the teacher or the audio narration says each name aloud, and the student points to or clicks the matching picture.

**Directions shown to student/facilitator:** "Listen to each name. Point to (or click) the picture that matches."

**Submission:** Facilitator marks the assignment complete once the student has matched all four pictures, in one sitting or across a few tries. No minimum score.

**Grading:** Full credit for completion. No partial credit distinctions — this is a completion-based Canvas assignment, not an auto-graded quiz.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What is Mount Vesuvius?
A) A Roman house
B) A volcano near the Bay of Naples in Italy
C) A type of Roman pottery
D) A Latin word for jewelry
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — Mount Vesuvius is a volcano in Italy, near the Bay of Naples."

**Q2.** What does the Latin word *mōns* mean?
A) House
B) Bread
C) Mountain
D) Jar
**Correct: C**
Feedback (correct): "Yes — mōns means mountain, as in mōns Vesuvius."

**Q3.** What does the Latin word *domus* mean?
A) House
B) Mountain
C) Painting
D) Jewelry
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Correct — domus means house, like the houses found preserved at Pompeii."

**Q4.** Why do historians and archaeologists know so much about daily life in Pompeii?
A) Because Roman writers described the city in great detail
B) Because the ash covered everything so completely that it preserved buildings, streets, and objects almost perfectly
C) Because the city was never actually buried
D) Because modern buildings were built exactly where Pompeii once stood
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Exactly — the thick ash acted almost like a time capsule, preserving the city in remarkable detail."

**Q5.** Where does the word "archaeology" come from?
A) Latin
B) Greek
C) Italian
D) Egyptian
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — archaeology comes from Greek, not Latin, even though it's the word we use for studying places like Pompeii."
