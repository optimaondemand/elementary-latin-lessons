# Roman Passport — Page 3 (Quarter 3 Milestone) — Module 24

**Teacher note (not shown to students):** This is the third of four planned Roman Passport milestone entries for the year (Page 1 in Quarter 1, Page 2 in Quarter 2, Page 3 here in Quarter 3, and a Quarter 4 showcase to close out the year).

## Foundations (K-2)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment (completion-based)
**Instructions:** This week, Session 2 is your special Roman Passport time instead of the usual quick check. Draw a picture of something you learned about Rome this quarter, then tell a grown-up one fact about your picture.
**What the student does:** The student draws a picture of something they learned this quarter — a color, a body word, something about school, the seasons, a Roman god, a phrase, Pompeii, or the Roman army — the student's choice, from any point in Quarter 3. The student then dictates one fact about the drawing to a grown-up, who writes or types the fact down (on the drawing itself, in a notebook, or directly into the Canvas Assignment comment box).
**Submission:** A photo of the drawing (with the grown-up's noted fact, if written on it) uploaded to the Canvas Assignment, or the fact typed into the assignment comment box by the grown-up on the student's behalf. Graded as complete/incomplete only — no correctness grade, no minimum length.

## Primary (3-5)
**Type:** Canvas Assignment (M365 Word doc, completion/light credit)
**Instructions:** Open your ongoing Roman Passport Word document (the same one you started in Module 1 and add to each quarter). Add a new entry labeled "Quarter 3." Draw or paste a simple image, write one fact you learned this quarter, and write one Latin word you learned this quarter along with what it means.
**What the student does:** In their Roman Passport M365 Word document, the student adds a dated Quarter 3 entry containing: (1) a drawn or pasted simple image, (2) one fact learned this quarter (about colors, body words, school, seasons, Roman gods, everyday phrases, Pompeii, or the Roman army), and (3) one Latin word learned this quarter with its meaning — the student's choice, from any Q3 module (for example, one of this module's words: <em>legiō</em> — a large, organized group of Roman soldiers, or <em>mīles</em> — a soldier).
**Submission:** The student saves the updated Roman Passport Word document to their course OneDrive folder and submits/links it via the Canvas Assignment. Graded as complete or light credit (e.g., a small completion-based point value) — not heavily graded for writing quality.
