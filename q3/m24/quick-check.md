## About the Cumulative Review Game

This is a fun, completion-based review — **not graded for correctness**, and not a new content assessment. It simply resamples words and ideas already taught this year to help students see how much they've learned. A teacher or facilitator can run it live (calling out a mixed set of prompts) or as a self-paced replay of familiar matching-game formats already built into earlier modules.

The review may draw from any of the following, as a fun illustrative sample (does not need to be exhaustive):

- Greetings (*salvē*, *valē*)
- Numbers and Roman numerals
- Family and food words
- Roman gods (Rounds I–III)
- Roads and aqueducts
- Colors in Latin
- Body words
- School words
- Everyday phrases

**Grading:** Completion-based only. There is no score, no correct/incorrect tally, and no minimum number of items a student must get "right" — the goal is a fun, low-pressure look back across the year so far, alongside the Roman Passport Page 3 entry.
