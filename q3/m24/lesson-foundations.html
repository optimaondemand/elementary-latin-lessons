Legiō! A Big, Organized Team

📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
A team of thousands, all working together!
Long ago, Roman soldiers worked together in a huge, organized group called a legiō. A legiō could have thousands of mīles (soldiers) in it! The group stayed so organized that everyone knew their job — kind of like a huge, well-run team. Today we say two new Latin words and sort pictures the Roman way: neat and organized!
🎯 BY THE END OF TODAY YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I can say two Latin words: legiō (a big, organized group of soldiers) and mīles (a soldier).
2
I can pick the picture that shows a neat, organized, Roman-style group.
3
I can match a picture of something the Roman army built to the words "the army built this."

▶  Go Further: build your own organized group
