# Module 22 Quick Check — Everyday Latin Phrases

**Teacher-facing note (not shown to students):** CPALMS/Florida World Languages standards for this module — WL.K12.NH.7.1, WL.K12.NH.8.2.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade attached).

**Activity — "Match the Word to the Picture":** Students see four pictures (a list ending in "...", a school building, a clipboard, a stop sign) alongside the four Latin words (*et cētera, alma māter, agenda, vēto*), presented as a picture-matching game (the same click-to-match widget style used in the lesson, or a teacher-led verbal version for a facilitator to run live). The student matches each picture to its spoken Latin word. No text reading is required — the teacher or the audio narration says each word aloud, and the student points to or clicks the matching picture.

**Directions shown to student/facilitator:** "Listen to each Latin word. Point to (or click) the picture that matches."

**Submission:** Facilitator marks the assignment complete once the student has matched all four words, in one sitting or across a few tries. No minimum score.

**Grading:** Full credit for completion. No partial credit distinctions — this is a completion-based Canvas assignment, not an auto-graded quiz.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What does *et cētera* mean?
A) I forbid
B) And the rest
C) Things to be done
D) Nourishing mother
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — et cētera means 'and the rest,' which is why it's shortened to 'etc.' at the end of a list."

**Q2.** What does *alma māter* mean, and how is the phrase used today?
A) "I forbid" — used when a law is rejected
B) "Things to be done" — used for a meeting's list of topics
C) "Nourishing mother" — used for the school a person graduated from
D) "And the rest" — used at the end of a list
**Correct: C**
Feedback (correct): "Yes — alma māter means 'nourishing mother,' and people use it for the school they graduated from."

**Q3.** What is an *agenda*?
A) A list of topics to be discussed at a meeting
B) A list of groceries to buy
C) A phrase said at graduation
D) A word a president uses to approve a law
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Correct — an agenda is a list of topics for a meeting, from the Latin for 'things to be done.'"

**Q4.** When a president or governor vetoes a proposed law, what are they doing?
A) Approving it
B) Rejecting or blocking it
C) Rewriting it
D) Reading it aloud
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — vēto means 'I forbid,' and it's used when a leader rejects or blocks a law."

**Q5.** Which of today's phrases is usually abbreviated as "etc."?
A) alma māter
B) agenda
C) vēto
D) et cētera
**Correct: D**
Feedback (correct): "Exactly — et cētera is almost always shortened to 'etc.' at the end of a list."
