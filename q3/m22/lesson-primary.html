📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why do grown-ups still say Latin words like "etc." and "veto"?
Latin stopped being anyone's everyday spoken language centuries ago, but a handful of Latin words and phrases never left. People still write them, say them, and hear them constantly — at school, in a meeting, on the news. Today you'll meet four of the most common ones and learn exactly where each is still used.

📖 PART 2 · READING: WORDS THAT NEVER LEFT
Latin died out as an everyday spoken language long ago, but many Latin words and phrases are still used every single day by ordinary people. At the end of a list, people write et cētera — "and the rest" — usually shortened to "etc." A person calls the school they graduated from their alma māter, meaning "nourishing mother." Before a meeting starts, someone hands out the agenda, a list of "things to be done." And when a president or governor rejects a law, they vēto it — the Latin word for "I forbid."
