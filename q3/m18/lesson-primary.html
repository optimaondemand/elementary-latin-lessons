📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why does a "manual" have anything to do with hands?
Doctors and scientists still use Latin words for parts of the body today, even though nobody speaks Latin at the dinner table anymore. Today you'll learn four Latin body words — and discover that three of them are still hiding inside English words you already use.

🩺 PART 2 · READING: WORDS THAT NEVER LEFT THE CLINIC
When a modern doctor examines a patient, she may still use words the Romans used for the body. Corpus meant "body," manus meant "hand," pēs meant "foot," and oculus meant "eye." Corpus is also an English word for a body of writing, and it's the root behind words like corporation — a company, or "body," of workers. Scientists and doctors kept these words because everyone, in every country, could agree on exactly what they meant.

🔍 PART 3 · DERIVATIVE HUNT

Three of today's body words are still hiding inside English words. Match each Latin word to the English word it helped create.

⚡ TRY IT · MATCH THE DERIVATIVE

2. pēs — "foot"
3. oculus — "eye"
🤗 NEED HELP?
Say each Latin word slowly and listen for it buried in the English choices. Manus sounds like the start of "manual." Pēs sounds like the start of "pedal." Oculus sounds like the start of "ocular."
