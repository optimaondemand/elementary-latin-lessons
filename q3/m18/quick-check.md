# Module 18 Quick Check — The Body in Latin

**Teacher-facing note (not shown to students):** CPALMS/Florida World Languages standards for this module — WL.K12.NM.8.1, WL.K12.NH.8.1.

---

## Foundations (K-2) — Canvas Assignment (completion-based)

**Type:** Canvas Assignment, completion grading only (no correctness grade attached).

**Activity — "Point to the Body Part" picture match:** Students see four pictures (a hand, a foot, an eye, and a whole body) alongside the four Latin words (*corpus, manus, pēs, oculus*), presented as a picture-matching/sorting game (the same click-to-match widget style used in the lesson, or a teacher-led verbal version for a facilitator to run live). The student matches each picture to its spoken Latin word. No text reading is required — the teacher or the audio narration says each word aloud, and the student points to or clicks the matching picture.

**Directions shown to student/facilitator:** "Listen to each Latin body word. Point to (or click) the picture that matches."

**Submission:** Facilitator marks the assignment complete once the student has matched all four body words, in one sitting or across a few tries. No minimum score.

**Grading:** Full credit for completion. No partial credit distinctions — this is a completion-based Canvas assignment, not an auto-graded quiz.

## Primary (3-5) — Canvas Quiz (auto-graded, 5 questions, multiple choice)

**Type:** Canvas Quiz, auto-graded, multiple choice, best of 2 attempts.

**Q1.** What does the Latin word *corpus* mean?
A) Body
B) Hand
C) Foot
D) Eye
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Right — corpus means body; it's also the root of English words like corporation, a company (or 'body') of workers."

**Q2.** What does the Latin word *oculus* mean?
A) Ear
B) Eye
C) Hand
D) Foot
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Yes — oculus means eye."

**Q3.** What does the Latin word *pēs* mean?
A) Foot
B) Hand
C) Body
D) Eye
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Correct — pēs means foot."

**Q4.** Which English word grew out of the Latin word *manus* ("hand")?
A) Manual
B) Mantle
C) Manner
D) Mammal
**Correct: A**
Feedback (correct): "Exactly — manual work is work done by hand, from manus."

**Q5.** Which English word grew out of the Latin word *oculus* ("eye")?
A) Optical
B) Ocular
C) Octagon
D) Occupy
**Correct: B**
Feedback (correct): "Right — ocular comes from oculus; an ocular exam is an eye exam."
