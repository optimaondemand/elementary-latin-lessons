Course Philosophy in One Paragraph

Elementary Latin builds cultural literacy and vocabulary reflexes, not grammar. There are no noun cases, no verb conjugation tables, and no declension paradigms in this course — that formal grammar sequence begins in M/J Latin Beginning (0706000) in 6th grade. Your job this year is to make Rome, Roman daily life, and the sound and shape of Latin words feel familiar and delightful, so that formal grammar feels like meeting an old friend rather than a foreign system when your students reach middle school Latin.

The Two-Lane Model

Every module ships two lesson-page variants covering the same weekly topic and vocabulary:

LaneAssign toWhat's different
FoundationsGrades K-2 (default)Picture/audio-led, no independent reading required, untimed games, spoken/drawn reflections
PrimaryGrades 3-5 (default)Short 2-4 sentence reading passages, derivative-hunt widgets, optional timed replay, typed reflections
You can override the default. Move a strong K-2 reader into Primary, or move a 3rd-5th grader who needs more support into Foundations. Nothing about the underlying topic changes — only the presentation depth.

Grading — What You Actually Enter in the Gradebook

  • Foundations Quick Checks: completion-based. The student engaged with the matching/sorting activity — mark complete. There is no "wrong" score to enter.
  • Primary Quick Checks: a 5-question auto-graded Canvas quiz, best of 2 attempts, per the module's quick-check.md spec.
  • Reflections: completion-based for both lanes. For Foundations, a parent/facilitator note that the student responded verbally or with a drawing is sufficient.
  • Roman Passport entries (x4/year): completion-based. Look for the drawing/fact/word — this is a keepsake project, not a graded essay.
There is no semester final exam in this course. The Module 33 Roman Passport Showcase is the closest thing to a summative event, and it is a celebration, not a test.

Handling Sensitive Content Age-Appropriately

A few modules touch topics that need a gentle hand:

  • M04 The Founding Story (Romulus & Remus) — told as a legend; the file does not dwell on violence.
  • M14 The Colosseum — framed as engineering and spectacle, not gladiatorial combat detail.
  • M23 Pompeii — framed around "the city was preserved, which is how we learn so much," not the human tragedy of the eruption.
  • M24 Roman Army Basics — framed around structure/organization and engineering (roads), not combat.

If a student asks a more detailed question about any of these topics, use your judgment about how much historical detail is appropriate for that individual child — the lesson content itself stays deliberately light-touch.

Accommodations

Every game defaults to untimed. Every reading passage in the Primary lane can be read aloud instead of read independently. Every reflection can be given verbally instead of typed. If a student has an IEP or 504 plan, no separate build is needed — the accommodation is simply "use the option that's already built in."

Standards Coverage

Each module's quick-check.md and reflection.md carry the relevant CPALMS codes (WL.K12.NM.x for Foundations-level proficiency, WL.K12.NH.x for Primary-level proficiency) as a teacher-facing note — these are not shown to students. Full mapping is in the course map (02_Design/course-map.html).
