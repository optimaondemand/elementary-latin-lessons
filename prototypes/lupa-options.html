Elementary Latin — Lupa (she-wolf) options

Three redrawn Lupa treatments for the founding legend of Rōmulus & Remus. The twins, seven hills, river, and fig tree are kept from the approved scene — only the wolf is changed so it unmistakably reads as a wolf: profile stance, tall pointed ears, long tapered snout, four slender legs, low tail. Pick one.

Choose one treatment

Option 1
Capitoline bronze

The famous statue look — bronze with patina-green shadows, a metallic sheen along the back, and a stylized plinth. Iconic and museum-like.

Option 2
Storybook gray wolf

A warm, friendly children's-book gray wolf — soft fur shading, cream muzzle and belly, gentle eye, head lowered over the twins. Clearly lupine, still tender.

Option 3
Flat-vector detailed

A clean modern flat wolf — no gradients, crisp shapes, a dark fur saddle over a light underside, and angular fur tufts at shoulder, haunch, and tail.

Current (rejected)

Why it failed: the muzzle is short and round, the ears are small and stubby, the legs merge into a single blob, and the body has no lean profile — it reads as a bear or generic critter, not a wolf. All three options above fix this with a long tapered snout, tall pointed ears, four distinct legs, and a lean arched profile.
